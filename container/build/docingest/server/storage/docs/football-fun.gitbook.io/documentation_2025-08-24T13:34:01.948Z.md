# Player Packs | Football.Fun

Player Packs are randomized packs that contain 4 players and award you varying number of shares. You can open up Player Packs using Tournament Points.

There's 3 tiers of packs available to open:

1.  **Pro Pack:** Smaller amount of shares per player
    
2.  **Epic Pack:** Medium amount of shares per player
    
3.  **Legendary Pack:** Large amount of shares per player
    

The cost of a pack is dynamically determined at the start of each tournament. This is to ensure that the distribution of players into the ecosystem aligns nicely with the earning of Tournament Points.

Your packs available will recharge every 4 hours, till you reach a maximum of 2 packs. Your Pack Balance can go above 2 if you're awarded packs from other means, such as objectives or tournament rewards.

Note: No more than 9 packs can be opened within a 24 hour period.

# The Currencies | Football.Fun

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/the-currencies#gold)

Gold


----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Gold is what is used to buy shares of players. You can earn gold by selling shares you own.

On Pro, Gold is 1:1 conversion with USDC (base).

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/the-currencies#fees)

Fees

The marketplace fees base rate are 5% per trade. However there is also variable fee structures in place for 2 scenarios.

1.  When trying to sell a large amount of supply compared to active supply, there is a 'Anti-dump mechanic' which triggers a higher tax, if trying to sell big supply in one transaction.
    
2.  When there is surge of trading activity on a player or abnormal activity on a player, the variable fee can kick in to help manage the pressure.

Trading Slippage

On our marketplace there's often a lot of trading activity happening at once. Slippage is when the price changes between the moment you press to start a trade and when the trade actually goes through.

You can adjust the slippage as you like. But if the price goes out of that range before your trade completes, it will revert the trade.

**Example:** You see 100 gold = 1,000 shares of Bellingham. With 10% slippage, you're saying "I'm okay with paying as high as 110 gold (10% extra) for those 1,000 if the price is moving whilst my trade is still finishing" And in this scenario if the price changes more than 10% (anything above 110 gold) the trade will not go through.

# How FDF Works | Football.Fun

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works#quickest-tldr)

Quickest TLDR

1.  Collect and Trade Player Shares to build up your squad
    
2.  Automatically enter Tournaments with active shares, with the goal of owning shares in players you think will perform the best in the upcoming Tournament.
    
3.  Win Tournament Points + Skill Points as Rewards from the Tournaments
    
4.  Open Player Packs with Tournament Points
    
    1.  Players go to your Development Squad
        
    
5.  Spend Skill Points to Promote Development Squad players to Active Squad, OR Cut them to discard them from Development Squad and receive a small return of Tournament Points.
    
6.  Flex & Have Fun
    
7.  Go back to 1.

# Contracts | Football.Fun

Each player also comes with 4 contracts by default. Shares that are in contract earn rewards for you in tournaments.

If your players shares contract expires to 0, the player will not be entered into the upcoming tournament for you and is not eligible to win you rewards.

You can extend contracts through your squad page **or** by purchasing more shares in a player.

Contracts only get used if a player in your squad enters the pitch in real life. If they do not play, no contract is used.

# Tournament Scoring - Basics | Football.Fun

Scoring is designed to give a true reflection of a player's real world performance.

Goals, Assists and Clean Sheets are crucial, however so is a strong overall contribution to the game. Just pick players who excel in their role in real life to win. You do not need to overthink it - if fans applaud it, the player probably got points for it. If fans are noticing misplaced passes or errors, they’ll probably lose some points too.

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/tournaments/tournament-scoring-basics#examples)

Examples

**Goalkeepers** score highly for clean sheets, saves, claiming the ball and even some for good distribution. But if they make mistakes they may struggle to get big points totals, even if they get a clean sheet.

**Defenders** who get a clean sheet and make a lot of tackles, interceptions whilst contributing to the build up play will usually perform better than a defender who got a clean sheet but otherwise had a quiet game.

**Midfielders** are generally expected to make a strong overall contribution to the game whilst getting a goal and/or assist to excel.

**Strikers** who score but also miss many big chances will get beaten by more clinical finishers. They’ll score higher when they make an all round contribution to play as well as just goals and assists.

# Tournaments | Football.Fun

Tournaments take place twice a week, with the Weekend Tournament running Friday to Monday, and the Midweek Tournament Tuesday to Thursday.

All players in your Active Squad with contracts take part in tournaments, there is no additional action to enter them into tournaments like setting a lineup in traditional fantasy. There’s 3 important things when it comes to tournament play.

1.  Your current amount of shares of each player. The more shares you have, the more rewards you get when the player wins.
    
2.  The total amount of active shares of each player in the tournament. The more widely owned a player is, the more people share the rewards. Therefore, the biggest wins are gained by picking unexpected or unknown players that win!
    
3.  Player Performance.
    

The top 5 scoring players in each outfield area of the pitch are rewarded at the end of tournaments. (Forwards, Midfielders, Defenders). The top 3 Goalkeepers are rewarded (but there are fewer of them, so less competition).

# Trading During Tournaments | Football.Fun

When a tournament is live there are features in place to ensure a good competitive spirit.

*   You can buy shares in a player right up to the start of their match, and as long as you buy before they enter the pitch (even as a sub), you will be eligible for rewards if they win.
    
*   When a player enters the pitch, the game takes a snapshot of who is holding them with an active contract.
    
*   If you sell shares after this point, you will not get the rewards if they win.
    
*   You can benefit if other holders of the player sell during the tournament, as this would increase the rewards distributed to the remaining holders.
    

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/tournaments/trading-during-tournaments#example)

Example

*   At time of snapshot you have 900 Valverde Shares
    
*   You buy 100 more shares after he starts the match -> Total holding = 1000 shares
    
*   You sell 50 shares -> No penalty (snapshot remained at 900)
    
*   You sell another 100 shares -> Snapshot drops to 850 and will no longer be able to increase back to the 900 original snapshot.
    

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/tournaments/trading-during-tournaments#subs)

Subs

Trading during an active tournament after players have already played is common strategy in attempt to maximise your rewards. To avoid users the ability to brute force with this method, you have 8 subs (rotating a player out of your squad mid tournament), before your skill points / skill rating are effected. Read more on it in the [Skill Rating / Skill Points](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/skill-rating-basic/skill-rating-advanced)
 page.

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/tournaments/trading-during-tournaments#trading-restriction)

**Trading Restriction**

One important thing to consider when trading during a tournament, is WHEN you can sell your winning players. [Tournament rewards](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/tournaments/tournament-prizes)
 and the trading restrictions are both distributed and unlocked at 4PM UTC on the day after the final tournament game.

# Tournament Prizes | Football.Fun

Tournament Rewards include [Tournament Points](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/the-currencies)
 & [Skill Points.](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/the-currencies)

Tournament Points are used to open Player Packs

Skill Points are part of a ranking system & are used to 'promote' development players

The top 5 outfield players per position will earn Tournament Points from the overall tournament prize pool.

*   Forwards
    
*   Midfielders
    
*   Defenders
    
*   Goalkeepers (have 3 winning places since there are far fewer of them).
    

The amount of rewards in a Tournament Reward pool is driven predominantly by the amount of volume traded on the platform. As more people play and trade, the reward pool grows.

Each player in the winning places will have an amount of Tournament Points available as rewards, which is then divided by the amount of shares of the player active.

Meaning if you hold 500 out of 1000 of the active shares for a player in rank 1, you will gain 50% of that prize.

What this means is, it is better to win with players that are less popular! The fewer people who own them, the more rewards you get when they win.

However there is no right or wrong way to play the game, nobody knows whether quietly holding the top players consistently or trading players chaotically is the best strategy yet, and they probably never will.

You can see the amount of shares you hold, against the total shares and predicted amount of Tournament Points won in the Live Scores page during tournaments.

# Skill Rating - Advanced | Football.Fun

This page will go into further detail about the breakdown of how Skill Rating is calculated and some of the variables of the system.

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/skill-rating-basic/skill-rating-advanced#tournament-score)

Tournament Score


------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TS is a measure of your efficiency in selecting high performing players in this particular Tournament:

Only your top 11 Players Performance Points during a tournament count towards your TS score.

This means you do not have to heavily optimise your squad for a particular tournament. If players don’t help your score, they don’t hurt it either.

All your players will earn a Tournament Score, so players who finish 7th or 8th for example can make a good contribution to your score. However, players that finish in the top 5 get boosted. The boost applied is after the base score the player got:

*   1st = ×2
    
*   2nd = ×1.5
    
*   3rd to 5th = ×1.25

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/skill-rating-basic/skill-rating-advanced#eligibility)

Eligibility

As mentioned above, the system is calculated on the top 11 performances in your squad.

To count towards TS, the player you own shares in must:

*   Be in your Active Squad
    
*   Have an active contract before their match
    
*   Be held before they enter the pitch and actually play
    

Any purchased player shares after kick off are excluded.

You should aim to have **at least 5 contracted players in your squad.** If you are below this threshold, you still receive Tournament Score, but it will be scaled down by 0.5x.

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/skill-rating-basic/skill-rating-advanced#substitutions)

Substitutions

There was a dominant tactic during the beta of rotating and trading players in the middle of a tournament. We want to retain the essence, but also adjust it so there's still more skill to your decisions and can't just be a brute force play method.

**You get 8 free subs per tournament**

When trading a player that has already played before the tournament has ended, this event triggers and Skill Rating (SR) penalties start to apply:

*   9-11 subs: SR x 0.9
    
*   12-14 subs: SR x 0.75
    
*   15+ subs: SR x0.3
    

Low volume trades <1% of your squad value will not trigger this event. Meaning micro sales of a player won't trigger a substitution.

There will be a warning on the trade window to make you aware if a Sub event will or will not trigger.

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/skill-rating-basic/skill-rating-advanced#skill-points-skill-leaderboard)

Skill Points / Skill Leaderboard

Each week, a pool of Skill Points is distributed based on your weekly Tournament Score.

The key rules are:

*   Bottom 25% of users by Tournament Score are excluded
    
*   Skill Points is awarded based on:
    
    *   Your Tournament Score of the total eligible players
        
    *   Your gold commitment size (if you hold larger amounts of shares, you earn more SP for the picks than someone who had the same picks but smaller size)
        
    
*   You must have a minimum Squad Value of 100 gold
    

Skill Points (SP) are a reward currency earned based on your performance TS each week.

They can be spent to promote players from the Development Pool to your Main Squad, thereby making them eligible for Tournaments and tradable..

Example: Player A vs Player B

*   If _Player A_ has a squad of 5 players, and all 5 players was in the top 5 selections.
    
    *   _Player A_ is seen as high skill.
        
    *   They made less picks to hit good outcomes.
        
    
*   If _Player B_ has a squad of 50 players, and also had only 5 players in the top 5 selections.
    
    *   _Player B_ is seen as less skilful than _Player A_.
        
    *   They had more decisions not hit good outcomes.

How to send invites


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Head to the Invite section on your profile pay, and there will be a copy invite button.

# FAQs | Football.Fun

What happens when a player runs out of contracts?[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/faqs#what-happens-when-a-player-runs-out-of-contracts)

If a player has 0 contracts, they are no longer 'active' and will not enter the upcoming tournaments, so you will not earn rewards should they place in the top 5s.

*   You can buy contracts again to make them active.
    
*   You can buy more shares. All new player shares start with 4 contracts. This works on a averaging system.
    
    *   If you have 1K shares with 2 contracts left, then buy another 1K shares (which have 4 contracts), you will end with 2K total shares, at 3 contracts.
        
    

*   You can sell players even if they have 0 contracts.
    

How do I get gold?[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/faqs#how-do-i-get-gold)

On the Pro version, you transfer USDC to your game wallet where it will appear as gold. On the Free to Play version, you start with 1K gold

How do I increase my squad value?[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/faqs#how-do-i-increase-my-squad-value)

Your squad value is the total value of all shares you own.

What if 2 players get the same score?[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/faqs#what-if-2-players-get-the-same-score)

*   Scores from OPTA are calculated to 2 decimal places. E.g. 192.15
    
*   If two players happened to get the same precise score, the mean of their last 5 scores would determine the winner ~ Better overall form is rewarded.
    
*   In the extra unlikely event that 2 players had the same exact score, the same exact form (almost impossible), then a random dice roll would determine the winner of the 2 players.
    

When do I get Tournament rewards?[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/faqs#when-do-i-get-tournament-rewards)

Tournament Rewards are always distributed at 4PM UTC on the following day from the LAST tournament game. Example: 14th 8pm UTC last game. Rewards will arrive 15th 4PM UTC.

# Tournament Scoring - Advanced | Football.Fun

This page sets out the details of the scoring system for advanced players! It is not necessary to understand all the detailed mechanics to play effectively, the [Tournament Scoring Basics](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/tournaments/tournament-scoring-basics)
 will be fine for most people.

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/tournaments/tournament-scoring-advanced#design-philosophy)

Design Philosophy


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The FDF scoring system is designed to capture the performance of players from the fan perspective. When you watch a game, fans applaud certain actions whilst others leave them howling in disbelief. Most fans just instinctively know what good and bad play looks like. These reactions vary depending on the player’s role. For example, a striker missing a big chance draws criticism, whereas defenders aren’t expected to be as clinical in front of goal. The FDF system accounts for these differences, rating each position (GK, DEF, MID, FWD) based on its typical expectations. We will continue refining the system, but our goal is for **FDF scores to feel natural and intuitive**, closely aligning with how most fans perceive performances. Of course, evaluating performance is subjective, and opinions will always differ—but we’ll get as close as possible.

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/getting-started/how-fdf-works/tournaments/tournament-scoring-advanced#scoring-system-detailed)

Scoring System - Detailed


---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A player's score for an action is made up of Base Points plus any specific Positional Points e.g.

*   Missing a **Big Chance** is **\-10** for any player, but **forwards receive an additional -5 penalty (total: -15)** since they are expected to convert these chances.
    
*   Defenders lose **\-5 points** for being dispossessed, while forwards only lose **\-1** because they take more risks in attack.

Wins and Draws


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Players get points for winning or drawing matches, with a small boost for winning in higher qualities of competition. This provides some balance between the different competitions. The maximum win bonus is 30, and the lowest is 10. For example, the **Champions League** is ranked higher than the **Europa League**, so players earn **+10 additional points** for a win in the Champions League. This ensures that, if two strikers perform identically but one does so in a tougher competition, the extra **+10 points** gives them the edge.

Competition rankings are based on **OPTA Power Rankings** (team strength based on real-life performances) and **club transfer market value**. These rankings will be updated periodically to reflect real-world shifts.

Big Match Modifier


---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When two teams with high **OPTA** [**Power Rankings**](https://theanalyst.com/2024/08/who-are-the-best-football-team-in-the-world-opta-power-rankings)
 face off, the match qualifies as a **Big Match**. Example:

*   If **Real Madrid** plays **Barcelona** in **La Liga**, the winning team receives the **maximum 30-point bonus** to reflect the game’s prestige.
    
*   If **PSG** faces a top club like **Lille, Lyon, or Monaco**, they would receive a **+20 win bonus**—the same as in La Liga.
    

The **maximum win bonus is capped at 30 points**—even if two top-tier clubs face off in the **Premier League** or **Champions League knockouts**, no additional boost is applied.

# Day 6 Recap | Football.Fun

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/dev-updates/day-6-recap#overview)

Overview

**Day 6 launch on FDF saw trading enabled for 10 new players in the market.**

And it was insane.

It was great to see so many people enjoy themselves, the chats, the trading, the frenzy of action, but whilst many had fun, a large number of you struggled to participate due to unavailability of our services.

We hear you. We felt it too.

This post is both a celebration of what we achieved together and a transparent look at why things went wrong, and how we’re making sure Day 7 is smoother.

* * *

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/dev-updates/day-6-recap#the-numbers)

**The Numbers**

Firstly, let’s just take a moment to appreciate the sheer scale of Day 6:

*   **Buys:** $702,925.18
    
*   **Sells:** $381,685.98
    
*   **Total Volume:** $1,084,611.15
    
*   **Transactions:** 11,080
    
*   **Requests served in 24h:** 16.27M
    
*   **Requests served between 17:30–20:00 alone:** 5.09M
    

That’s staggering growth in just 24 hours.

It feels strange to write a postmortem when the numbers are this strong, but as you know, being early comes with both upside and it’s personally frustrating that a large number of you had issues.

* * *

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/dev-updates/day-6-recap#the-lead-up)

**The lead up**

Seeing the massive increase in user numbers leading up to the launch and deposits into users wallets growing, the team had been proactively increasing resource capacity. In every previous launch we had seem similar traffic spikes. Whilst performance had generally been good across the system we were still observing weird behaviour with the containers during the initial spike in api traffic.

Our containers would have wide spread liveness probe checks. This health check ensures that a container is healthy by our orchestration system. When containers are considered unhealthy, they get restarted.

The combination of user growth + this issue we were seeing when under heavy load lead to our decision to do two things for launch day 7.

1.  Increase api workload capacity by 20%
    
2.  Increase the health check timeout seconds to 3s.
    

* * *

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/dev-updates/day-6-recap#what-happened)

**What Happened**

Plain and simple: **our systems weren’t keeping up with your demand.**

Every pod in our API layer was being restarted constantly. From your perspective, that looked like slow responses, timeouts, and failed trades.

*   17:00 - launch is delayed. We were observing an increase in container restarts and massive load on database
    
*   17:12 - Announcement is made to push back launch by 30 mins
    
*   17:20 - Api health resumes. Team was unable to explain the huge spike in database re-use. We speculated that not showing the new players early in transfers list was resulting in people hitting refresh a lot, creating more and more demand on DB. Database load comes down and traffic spike levels out.
    
*   17:30 - Players go live and we see massive spike in demand. Containers begin restarting constantly
    
*   18:00 - Team identifies potential issue with readiness check.
    
*   18:05 - Team applies patch to fdf-api deployment and starts roll out
    
*   18:07 - Rollout of new api config is deployed across all replicas. Restart rates drop to 0
    

![](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3171458711-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FJmqoGkXKv6dUk67EJq4E%252Fuploads%252FqaVfT3jYK8XeZcsOZnVO%252Fimage-20250822-062746.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3Df2dde5ee-f6df-47b2-9c67-1142b306093b&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=1fa6d57&sv=2)

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/dev-updates/day-6-recap#why-simplified)

Why? (simplified)


------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Because of a **misconfigured health check.**

*   Kubernetes (our orchestration layer) checks each API server’s health to see if it’s alive.
    
*   We left the default **1-second timeout** in place. That meant if your API call took just over 1s (for example because of a busy database or network blip), Kubernetes assumed the whole container was dead.
    
*   As load spiked, some responses slipped past the 1s mark → probes failed → Kubernetes marked pods unhealthy → pods restarted → traffic shifted to fewer pods → **feedback loop of death** 💀
    

We were seeing a large number of trades go through the system during the period where service was unavailable, but it was a case of pot luck. If you’re requests landed on pods before too much traffic hit the pod and was marked unhealthy, your trades would make it through.

* * *

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/dev-updates/day-6-recap#the-fix)

**The Fix**

**Extended health check timeouts** from 1s → 5s, and adjusted failure thresholds so pods tolerate small hiccups without being killed.

This change instantly resulted in a much more stable system under load. I believe that this issue had been present across all launches and is the reason we’d been seeing the spike in contain restart rates under load. The changes we made in preparation for more traffic magnified the problem.

* * *

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/dev-updates/day-6-recap#looking-ahead-to-day-7)

**Looking Ahead to Day 7**

Day 6 showed us two things:

1.  **The community’s energy is real.** Over $1M in volume in one day is no small feat.
    
2.  **We need to harden the platform faster.** Scaling issues aren’t glamorous, but fixing them now means a smoother experience for everyone going forward.
    

Remember, we’re playing with live ammo here in the second week of launching the biggest app on Base.

Day 7 is coming, and it will be better.

See you on the pitch.

— **The FDF Team**

Why? (simplified)


------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Because of a **misconfigured health check.**

*   Kubernetes (our orchestration layer) checks each API server’s health to see if it’s alive.
    
*   We left the default **1-second timeout** in place. That meant if your API call took just over 1s (for example because of a busy database or network blip), Kubernetes assumed the whole container was dead.
    
*   As load spiked, some responses slipped past the 1s mark → probes failed → Kubernetes marked pods unhealthy → pods restarted → traffic shifted to fewer pods → **feedback loop of death** 💀
    

We were seeing a large number of trades go through the system during the period where service was unavailable, but it was a case of pot luck. If you’re requests landed on pods before too much traffic hit the pod and was marked unhealthy, your trades would make it through.

* * *

### 

[](https://football-fun.gitbook.io/wiki/dev-updates#the-fix)

**The Fix**

**Extended health check timeouts** from 1s → 5s, and adjusted failure thresholds so pods tolerate small hiccups without being killed.

This change instantly resulted in a much more stable system under load. I believe that this issue had been present across all launches and is the reason we’d been seeing the spike in contain restart rates under load. The changes we made in preparation for more traffic magnified the problem.
