Token Verification
==================

Get your token verified on LORE for enhanced visibility and a trusted badge.

LORE Token Verification is a way for token creators to stand out on the platform. Verified tokens receive an emerald badge, priority placement in Live Calls, and enhanced visibility across the terminal. Verification signals to the community that the team behind a token is invested in its presence on LORE.

![LORE token verification page](https://lore.trade/images/screens/verify.png)

What You Get
------------

When a token is verified on LORE, it receives several visibility boosts across the platform:

VB

#### Verified Badge

An emerald verified icon appears next to your token's name everywhere it's displayed — dashboard, Live Calls, Vision, token cards, and the token detail page.

LC

#### Live Calls Priority

Verified tokens are pinned to the Live Calls ticker at the top of the app, giving your token persistent visibility alongside the hottest movers.

VS

#### Vision Boost

In the Vision screener, verified tokens are sorted above unverified tokens in the same column — appearing higher in the feed.

SG

#### Signal Broadcast

Verification triggers a signal across the LORE ecosystem — Discord and Telegram bots broadcast the verification to subscribed channels.

How to Verify
-------------

### Step 1 — Find Your Token

Navigate to your token's page on LORE. You can find it through the dashboard, Vision screener, or by searching. Your token must be a graduated token (from PumpFun) to be eligible for verification.

Click the **Verify** button in the token's header bar or hero section. This opens the verification flow.

### Step 2 — Connect Your Wallet

If you haven't already, connect your Solana wallet. Any wallet can submit a verification — it doesn't need to be the token creator's wallet.

### Step 3 — Add Token Info (Optional)

You can optionally add or update your token's information during the verification process:

*   **Description** — A short description of your token and project
*   **Website** — Your project's website URL
*   **X (Twitter)** — Your project's X account
*   **Telegram** — Community Telegram group
*   **Discord** — Community Discord server

If your token already has social links from DexScreener, they'll be pre-filled. You can update them or leave them as-is.

### Step 4 — Choose Payment

Select your payment method — SOL or LORE tokens:

| Payment Method | Cost | Duration |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **SOL** | 0.5 SOL | 30 days |
| **LORE Token** | 500,000 LORE | 30 days |

### Step 5 — Confirm Transaction

Click the pay button and approve the transaction in your wallet. The payment is sent to LORE's treasury wallet. Once the transaction confirms on-chain, the system automatically verifies the payment and activates the verified badge.

You'll see a success screen with a receipt including the transaction signature and a link back to your token's page — now with the verified badge active.

Where the Badge Appears
-----------------------

Once verified, the emerald badge shows up everywhere your token is displayed on LORE:

*   **Dashboard** — Token cards show an emerald overlay and badge
*   **Live Calls** — Verified tokens get an emerald ring and are pinned to the ticker
*   **Vision** — Verified tokens sort above unverified in the screener columns
*   **Token detail page** — The "Verify" button is replaced with a "Verified" badge
*   **Feature boxes** — If your token lands in a feature box, the verified badge is visible
*   **Signal bots** — Discord and Telegram signal messages include the verified status

Verification Rules
------------------

*   **One active verification per token** — You can't stack multiple verifications on the same token
*   **30-day duration** — Verification lasts 30 days from the moment of payment. After expiry, you can re-verify
*   **Any wallet can verify** — You don't need to be the token creator. Community members can verify tokens they support
*   **On-chain payment** — The transaction is verified on-chain. No refunds once the transaction confirms
*   **Graduated tokens only** — The token must have graduated from PumpFun to be eligible

NOTE

LORE Verification is separate from DexScreener's paid profile. A token can have both — DexScreener verification appears as "Dex Paid" in the token data, while LORE verification gives the emerald badge and visibility boosts within the LORE platform.

Verification vs. DexScreener
----------------------------

| Feature | LORE Verification | DexScreener Paid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Platform** | LORE terminal and signal bots | DexScreener website |
| **Badge** | Emerald verified icon | "Dex Paid" label |
| **Payment** | SOL or LORE to LORE treasury | Paid through DexScreener |
| **Visibility** | Priority in Live Calls, Vision, signal broadcasts | Enhanced profile on DexScreener |
| **Duration** | 30 days | Varies |

TIP

For maximum visibility, combine both. A token with LORE verification and a DexScreener paid profile gets the best of both platforms — the emerald badge and priority placement on LORE, plus enhanced presence on DexScreener.

Checking Verification Status
----------------------------

You can check any token's verification status directly from its token page. The header shows either a "Verify" button (not yet verified) or a "Verified" badge with the expiry date.

Your verification history is also available in your wallet's profile — showing all tokens you've verified, their current status, and expiry dates.

The Trading Bots
----------------

A trading bot is an independent agent with its own Solana wallet, exit strategy, and trade history. The system can manage multiple bots simultaneously — each operating autonomously with different configurations.

### What Each Bot Has

*   **Dedicated wallet** — Every bot trades from its own Solana wallet, keeping funds and positions fully isolated
*   **Exit strategy** — Configurable take-profit, stop-loss, trailing stops, moon bags, and re-entry rules
*   **Signal filters** — Which LORE signal types and box categories the bot responds to
*   **Strategy preset** — Battle-tested configurations optimized for different market patterns
*   **Trade history** — Complete record of every buy, sell, and auto-exit for performance analysis

Position Management
-------------------

Once a bot enters a trade, the auto-exit engine takes over. Positions are monitored continuously and managed through several exit mechanisms:

*   **Take-profit** — Sells the majority of the position when the price rises above a configured target
*   **Stop-loss** — Exits immediately if the price drops below a safety threshold to limit losses
*   **Trailing stop** — Tracks the peak price and exits if the token drops a percentage from that peak, letting winners run while protecting gains
*   **Moon bags** — On take-profit, keeps a small percentage as a zero-risk position that rides for potential further upside
*   **Dip re-entry** — After a profitable exit, watches for a dip and buys back in if conditions are right

Moon bags are a signature feature. Instead of selling 100% on take-profit, the system keeps a small slice — the profit has already been banked, so this remaining position rides risk-free. If the token continues running, the moon bag captures the upside. If it dumps, nothing is lost.

Dynamic Strategy Adaptation
---------------------------

Exit strategy parameters are not static. The system adjusts thresholds in real time based on the characteristics of each individual token:

*   **Market cap scaling** — Smaller tokens are more volatile and get wider thresholds; larger tokens get tighter exits
*   **Volatility scaling** — Recent price action determines how aggressive or conservative the stops are
*   **Volume-aware stops** — If trading volume is thin, stop-losses widen to avoid getting stopped out by low liquidity
*   **Trend-based exits** — The system detects dangerous patterns like dump events, dying momentum, liquidity drains, and distribution

Self-Learning System
--------------------

The trading agents don't just trade — they learn. Every trade feeds into a learning pipeline that analyzes performance patterns and adapts the strategy over time.

TP

#### Trade Pattern Recognition

Groups past trades by token, market conditions, and exit type. Identifies which conditions produce the best results and which to avoid.

MR

#### Market Regime Detection

Classifies the current market as trending, ranging, or volatile. Each regime has different optimal parameters.

AT

#### Adaptive Tuning

Based on recent performance, suggests and applies parameter adjustments — widening targets on winning streaks, tightening stops on losing streaks.

LP

#### LORE Signal Analysis

Tracks which LORE signal types and box categories perform best, informing which signals are most worth trading.

Adaptations only apply when the system's confidence score exceeds a minimum threshold, preventing overreaction to small sample sizes. The learning system gets smarter with every trade, building a persistent knowledge base that survives restarts.

Wallet Copy Trading
-------------------

In addition to LORE signals, the trading agents can follow other Solana wallets and automatically mirror their trades. This is useful for replicating the entries of successful traders while still applying the system's own exit strategy and risk management.

*   **Copy Buys mode** — Copies the buy side only, then uses the bot's own exit strategy to manage the position
*   **Mirror Trades mode** — Copies both buys and sells, fully trusting the followed wallet's judgment
*   **Safety checks** — Minimum volume, maximum downtrend, and buy pressure thresholds prevent copying into bad conditions
*   **LORE safety gate** — In mirror mode, the system can require that a token is LORE-approved before copying, falling back to Copy Buys mode if not

Trading Conditions Monitor
--------------------------

The system continuously monitors market-wide conditions to inform trading decisions:

| Condition | What It Measures |
| --- | --- |
| **Heat** | Overall market activity level — from slow to hot |
| **Buy Pressure** | Ratio of buys to sells across the market |
| **Saturation** | How many new tokens are launching — from open to saturated |
| **Call Success** | How well recent LORE calls have performed |

These conditions feed into the bots' dynamic strategy adjustments, helping them trade more conservatively in hostile conditions and more aggressively when the market is favorable.

Scout Swarm

How LORE's Scout Agent discovers and delivers the best tokens to the feature boxes.

The three feature boxes at the top of the LORE dashboard — **Highest Gainer**, **Fastest Mover**, and **Gamble Pick** — are not static displays. They are powered by a dedicated selection system called the **Scout Swarm**, which continuously evaluates every active token and surfaces the most relevant candidates in real time.

This is one of LORE's most important systems. While the scanning pipeline analyzes individual tokens, the Scout Swarm looks across the entire pool to decide which tokens deserve the spotlight right now.

Discovery & Filtering
---------------------

The pipeline starts with a **discovery scan** — pulling every active graduated token from the database. This pool can contain hundreds of tokens at any given time. Before any selection happens, the Scout Agent applies a series of global filters to ensure quality and safety:

*   **Market fundamentals** — Minimum thresholds for market cap, liquidity, and trading volume
*   **Holder distribution** — Checks for healthy holder counts and distribution patterns
*   **Anti-manipulation** — Filters out tokens showing signs of artificial pump activity or wash trading
*   **Risk assessment** — Flags tokens with suspicious bundle activity or concentrated supply

Only tokens that pass all global filters advance to the candidate pools. This ensures the feature boxes never highlight a token with obvious red flags.

The Three Boxes
---------------

After filtering, the Scout Agent builds separate candidate pools for each feature box. Each box has its own selection criteria and ranking logic:

### Highest Gainer

Surfaces the token with the strongest price performance. The Scout Agent ranks candidates by price momentum and selects the top performer. This box answers the question: _"What's moving the most right now?"_

*   Ranked by price change over recent time windows
*   Must sustain momentum — not just a single-candle spike
*   Held for a short duration before rotation to the next top performer

### Fastest Mover

Highlights tokens experiencing rapid acceleration. Unlike the Highest Gainer which looks at total performance, the Fastest Mover focuses on tokens that are gaining speed — catching momentum as it builds.

*   Focuses on short-timeframe velocity rather than total gain
*   Maintains a pool of multiple candidates you can cycle through
*   Rotates frequently to stay current with fast-moving conditions

### Gamble Pick

The most dynamic box. The Gamble Pick deliberately surfaces tokens that are earlier in their lifecycle — higher risk, but higher potential. The candidate pool is shuffled to provide variety and surface tokens you might not discover otherwise.

*   Broader selection criteria — designed for discovery
*   Pool is shuffled to avoid recency bias
*   Shortest rotation window — refreshes often

RISK

The Gamble Pick is intentionally higher risk. Tokens in this box may have lower liquidity or fewer holders than those in the other two boxes. Always do your own research before acting on any featured token.

Deduplication & Rotation
------------------------

A key rule of the Scout Swarm: **no token can appear in more than one box at the same time.** The selection runs in priority order — Highest Gainer first, then Fastest Mover, then Gamble Pick — and each step excludes tokens already chosen.

Tokens don't stay in feature boxes forever. Each box has a maximum display duration, after which the token is rotated out and a new candidate takes its place. This keeps the feature boxes fresh and ensures new opportunities surface continuously.

*   **Highest Gainer** — Longest hold time, giving you time to evaluate top performers
*   **Fastest Mover** — Medium hold time, balancing freshness with usability
*   **Gamble Pick** — Shortest hold time, maximizing discovery

If a featured token becomes inactive, gets removed, or no longer meets the minimum criteria, it is exited from the box immediately — without waiting for the timer.

AI Enrichment Layer
-------------------

After selection, each featured token is enriched with an additional AI analysis layer. The Scout Swarm's AI component provides:

*   **Sentiment analysis** — Real-time sentiment scoring from social and market signals
*   **Risk profile** — An independent risk assessment overlaid on the token's base data
*   **Performance context** — How the token's current run compares to historical patterns
*   **Smart candidate tagging** — Background AI tagging that feeds into future selection cycles

This enrichment happens after selection, so it doesn't slow down the core pipeline. The AI layer adds depth to what you see in the feature boxes without affecting which tokens get chosen.

Real-Time Delivery
------------------

Once a token is selected and enriched, the Scout Swarm broadcasts the update to every connected client via WebSocket. Feature box changes appear instantly — no page refresh needed. Between full selection cycles, the system also refreshes metrics (price, volume, gains) on a shorter interval, keeping the displayed data accurate even between rotations.

TIP

The feature boxes are one of the fastest ways to spot what's trending right now. Watch for rapid rotations in the Gamble Pick — it often signals a burst of new activity in the market.

*   **Execution data** — Every trade the bots made — entry price, exit price, hold duration, PnL, exit reason, and the market conditions at the time
*   **Portfolio state** — Open and closed positions, realized PnL, and bot performance metrics

Visual Inspection

Every trade is visualized on professional candlestick charts with the bot's executions overlaid directly on the price action:

*   **Buy markers** — Green circles at the exact time and price of each entry
*   **Sell markers** — Red circles at each exit point
*   **AI annotations** — Diamond-shaped pins from the AI review: missed exits in gold, good trades in green, bad entries in red, held-too-long moments in orange

This makes it immediately visible what happened — you can see at a glance whether the bot entered too late, exited too early, missed a re-entry opportunity, or nailed the trade.

Dataset Creation
----------------

Every analyzed trade is captured as a structured snapshot with three sections:

### Pre-Trade Conditions

What the market looked like before the bot entered — price momentum, volatility, volume profile, and the raw candle data leading up to the entry. This captures the conditions the bot was reading when it made the decision.

### Post-Trade Outcome

What happened after the bot entered — peak price and time to peak, price at multiple time horizons (5 minutes, 15 minutes, 1 hour, 24 hours), drawdown from peak, and the full price action after entry.

### Trade Results

The bot's actual performance — PnL, hold duration, exit reason, number of buys and sells, and which bots were involved. Combined with the AI's outcome classification and any human verdicts from the team.

These snapshots accumulate into a growing dataset that makes it possible to ask questions like: _"What pre-trade conditions do the best trades have in common?"_ or _"When we get stopped out, what does the volatility look like beforehand?"_

Strategy Generation
-------------------

The final stage of the pipeline. The system synthesizes all accumulated analysis data — AI findings, chart annotations, dataset patterns — and generates optimized strategy presets for the trading bots.

*   **Pattern clustering** — Groups findings by market pattern (high volatility, pump-and-dump, sustained momentum, parabolic moves)
*   **AI synthesis** — Sends the clustered patterns to AI to generate tuned strategy configurations for each pattern type
*   **Preset output** — Produces complete bot configurations with optimized take-profit, stop-loss, trailing stop, moon bag, and re-entry parameters

These generated strategies are battle-tested against historical data before being deployed to live bots. The cycle then repeats — the bots trade with the new strategies, those trades get reviewed, and the strategies improve further.

TIP

The training system creates a continuous improvement loop. Every trade the bots make — win or loss — adds to the dataset that makes the next generation of strategies smarter. The longer the system runs, the more refined the trading becomes.

Human-in-the-Loop

While the system is heavily automated, human judgment plays a key role. Analysts can add verdicts and notes to any trade or token launch, providing context the AI can't capture on its own:

*   **Trade verdicts** — Good trade, bad trade, risky, lucky, missed opportunity
*   **Launch labels** — Organic, bundled, sniped, rug
*   **Notes** — Free-text observations that enrich the dataset

This human feedback becomes part of the training data, creating a labeled dataset that combines automated analysis with experienced judgment. Over time, this makes the AI's pattern recognition increasingly accurate.

*   **Token identity** — Name, symbol, and contract address
