Defi Dungeons is a fantasy idle-MMORPG built on Solana. From MMORPG features to on-chain DeFi systems, DeFi Dungeons offer an exciting realm to explore.

[](#the-game)

The Game


---------------------------

Set in the warcraft-inspired town of Nightvale, users progress from Novice Adventurers to Fabled Raiders. From bountiful dungeons to challenging raids, group up and explore all DeFi Dungeons has to offer while searching for $GOLD and progressing your Heroes.

Blending concepts from your favourite on-chain idle games with traditional RPGs like WoW and Runescape into Solana’s newest game-fi experience.

Defi Dungeons is built by Longwood Labs, the team that brought you [The Heist](https://x.com/playtheheist)
.

[](#the-vision)

The Vision


-------------------------------

After 3 years of live development across multiple successful Web3 titles, DeFi Dungeons is the culmination of Longwood Labs' experience & founders' play time across nostalgic RPG favorites. We aim to bring a fresh RPG experience to the blockchain with our signature idle gaming systems built in. DeFi Dungeons is built for all players, from those who want to play multiple times per day to those who want to play passively once a week. DeFi Dungeons focuses on a scalable, replayable gameplay loop, built for fun, progression & reward.

# Adventurer | DeFi Dungeons

Hero NFT Mint 31st March on Magic Eden.

[PreviousHeroes](/defi-dungeons/heroes)
[NextBanker](/defi-dungeons/heroes/banker)

Last updated 4 days ago

The Adventurer is the most active character in DeFi Dungeons, with access to all features and in-depth progression and game systems.

Adventurers go on Quests, slay bosses in Dungeons & Raids, and train up in the training ground to progress through Nightvale.

### 

[](#classes)

Classes

The Adventurer is divided into 3 classes: the Warrior, the Mage, & the Marksman. Each class specializes in different weaponry and armor types, but all share the same goal: to fill their pouches with $GOLD.

In end-game content, the attack style of each class will affect your efficiency against certain bosses. This creates the need for correct composition in certain features. There are 3584 Warriors, 3583 Marksmen, & 3583 Mages.

* * *

#### 

[](#combat-level)

Combat Level

An Adventurer's combat level determines the rewards they receive in Quests and their chances of completing Dungeons and Raids. Combat Level is dependent on your stat points and your equipped items.

Combat Level calculation: Cumulative Power (base and equipment) + Cumulative Vitality (base and equipment) / 4

Hero progression will be staggered throughout expansions. A new combat level cap with all new Items, Dungeons, and Raids will be released each time a new expansion is released. The current Combat Level cap is 100 on launch.

# Dungeon Mechanics | DeFi Dungeons

Hero NFT Mint 31st March on Magic Eden.

[PreviousDungeons](/the-game/dungeons)
[NextQuests](/the-game/quests)

Last updated 4 days ago

Each Dungeon has different Odds, Bosses, Risks, and Rewards

#### 

[](#dungeon-instance)

Dungeon Instance

Each Dungeon consists of:

*   A Notice Board that displays Dungeon information/ loot tables
    
*   The Dungeon Boss to battle
    
*   A chest to claim your loot
    

* * *

#### 

[](#how-do-dungeons-work)

How do Dungeons work?

1.  Begin by clicking on the Boss in the Dungeon instance. You must have the required Dungeon key to enter the Dungeon.
    
2.  Users must equip their characters in preparation for the dungeon. You can do this in the dungeon send screen or in the my heroes section.
    
3.  Start the Dungeon after equipping and selecting your characters.
    
4.  Dungeons take 15 minutes to complete.
    
5.  Once the timer ends, users can claim to see if they killed the Boss and what rewards they earned from the Dungeon run.
    
6.  Users can equip newly found Equipment to improve their combat level.
    
7.  Users can sell newly acquired loot at the Loot Exchange for $GOLD.
    

* * *

#### 

[](#dungeon-keys)

Dungeon Keys

Each Dungeon requires its own unique entry key. Adventurers use 1 Dungeon Key per Dungeon Run. Keys are found while Questing and are bound to the account, not the individual NFT.

* * *

#### 

[](#combat-level-and-dungeon-difficulty)

Combat Level & Dungeon difficulty

Each Dungeon increases in its difficulty. Each with a higher recommended Combat Level than the prior, but with rarer and more valuable rewards. The adventurers' chance to kill each Boss varies based on their combat level vs the Boss's combat level. Adventurers can attempt any boss at any combat level, but trying to kill a boss much higher than your level is risky and likely to fail. Adventurers can see their exact % chance to kill each boss when their NFT is selected in the Dungeon Send screen.

If you are above or below the recommended combat level suggested for the Dungeon, your odds to kill may drastically change to represent your power vs the boss. For example, if you try to kill The Inquisitor at level 1, the odds of success will be sub 1%.

Dungeons 5 & above (level 100) are the only Dungeons where you will have a chance of **Death**. If you are unfortunate enough to hit the Death event, your Adventurer NFT will be burnt, and his remains will be scattered in Nightvales Graveyard, never to return.

* * *

#### 

[](#loot-table)

Loot Table

Each Boss has a Different Loot table. The odds for each piece of equipment and loot can be found in the Loot table of each boss. Each class can earn different Equipment from the boss, but all share the same 'Loot' pool.

#### 

[](#the-quest-master)

The Quest Master

Adventurers begin questing at the Quest Master in Nightvale Square. Quest Trips are perpetual, meaning you can stay Questing for as long as you like, and your rewards will stack up.

This allows users to play DeFi Dungeons at their own pace. You can claim all of your Quests once per week, or can claim daily or hourly.

* * *

#### 

[](#how-do-quests-work)

How do Quests work?

1.  Access the Quest UI from the Quest Master
    
2.  Select & Equip Adventurers you would like to Quest
    
3.  Complete 1 Quest per hour. Each time a user completes a Quest, they have a chance to earn rewards from the Quest Reward Table.
    
4.  Claim Rewards at any time.
    
5.  Earn Dungeon Keys and Merchant Loot
    
6.  Use Dungeon Keys to enter Dungeons, and sell your Loot for $GOLD.
    

* * *

#### 

[](#quest-rewards-table)

Quest Rewards Table

The Quest Reward Table is Combat Level dependent, and changes as you progress through the game, to ensure your rewards are representative of your power.

# Economy & Expansions | DeFi Dungeons

Hero NFT Mint 31st March on Magic Eden.

[PreviousReferrals](/the-game/referrals)

Last updated 4 days ago

### 

[](#expansions)

Expansions

DeFi Dungeons will take an expansion-based approach to new content & game scaling.

The first expansion allows users to reach a level of 140 and will include 6 Dungeons and 2 Raids.

With each new expansion, we introduce new content (dungeons, raids, gear) and increase the level cap. This model allows us to scale up progression indefinitely without having the need for economic resets at each expansion. This works due to our faucet model for Emissions, which ensures emissions stay constant and do not become overinflationary.

* * *

### 

[](#economy)

Economy

The $GOLD faucet model ensures that only a certain number of tokens are given out per day, spread out across the economy. As all emissions come from the Loot Exchange Faucet and the loot items have weightings, as users progress through the game, earlier items become worth less than end-game items. This creates a dynamic where users have to progress with the player base to stay competitive with their $GOLD earnings.

# Loot Exchange | DeFi Dungeons

Hero NFT Mint 31st March on Magic Eden.

[PreviousThe Tavern](/the-game/the-tavern)
[NextBanking](/the-game/the-tavern/banking)

Last updated 4 days ago

Sell loot for $GOLD at the Loot Exchange.

Users sell their Loot for $GOLD at the Loot Exchange. This is the main faucet of $GOLD in the game and where Heroes come to realise their hard work. Bankers take a cut on each transaction in the Loot Exchange.

* * *

### 

[](#pricing-model)

Pricing model

Each loot item has a weighted value. Each day, the Merchant will emit a set amount of $GOLD between all loot sales. It calculates the value of each loot item by using a weighted moving average of the past 12 hours of sales data. This model aims to create a cap on daily emissions but promote game progression by creating a model where users earn more of the daily emissions by selling higher tier loot. This creates a dynamic where users must progress to end-game content to earn their share of the daily emissions. A user cannot sit at early game content earning common items and earning a large share of the emissions.

**Working example:** Let's say there are 100 Broken Skulls (with a weighting of 1) and 50 Adventurer Books (with a weighting of 2) traded in during a 24-hour period. The Merchant would distribute 1,000,000 $GOLD\* among all item sales in proportion to their weightings. In this example, each Broken Skull would be worth 5,000, and each Adventurer Book would be worth 10,000. The working example overly simplifies the reality of the algorithm, and there may be edge cases and simulation used to create the environment described (1,000,000 tokens emitted per day spread between all loot item sales).

\*example
