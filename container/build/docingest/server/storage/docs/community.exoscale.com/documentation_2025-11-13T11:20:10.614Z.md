Exoscale
========

What is Exoscale?[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/exoscale/#what-is-exoscale)

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Exoscale is a European cloud provider focusing on simplicity and security.

Who are we?[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/exoscale/#who-are-we)

----------------------------------------------------------------------------

We are a startup founded in 2011 in Lausanne, Switzerland. Our goal is to provide simple, scalable, yet affordable cloud infrastructure services from safe locations.

Our service offerings[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/exoscale/#our-service-offerings)

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Exoscale offers four types of cloud services:

### Infrastructure as a Service[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/exoscale/#infrastructure-as-a-service)

Securely host your data with our end-to-end encrypted Database as a Service (DBaaS), allowing you to set up and scale databases in minutes without the need for costly hardware or licenses. Avoid the hassle of on-premises solutions and speeding up your time to market for new developments. Pay only for what you use with Exoscale DBaaS, cutting down both administration and maintenance overheads. Discover Database as a Service with our [DBaaS quickstart guide](https://community.exoscale.com/product/dbaas/quick-start/)
.

### DNS[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/exoscale/#dns)

When you need to deploy an instance or an application, you want it to be accessible in a simple and reliable way. Our DNS service allows you to control your domain name and its records from [the Portal](https://community.exoscale.com/product/networking/dns/how-to/config-examples/)

# Quick Start – Exoscale Documentation

Quick Start
===========

To start using Exoscale’s services, you will need to create an account and at least one [Organization](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/organization/)
. Think about an Organization as an isolated workspace where you can invite people and store your resources.

Registration[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/quick-start/#registration)

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

To register your account, go to our [account registration page](https://portal.exoscale.com/register/)
.

The process will guide you through:

1.  Verify your email address
2.  Setup a new Organization
3.  Register your credit card, or add some credits in advance

Once your registration is complete, you will land in our web portal and be able to start operating on the different services composing the platform.

### Register as a Company[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/quick-start/#register-as-a-company)

During the registration process you can choose to identify yourself as a Company.

This will allow you to register your European or Swiss VAT number during the registration process. Additionally, established companies with a proved line of credit can request access to post-paid billing with [wire transfer](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#post-paid-billing-with-wire-transfer)
 after a manual vetting process.

### Free Trial Credit[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/quick-start/#free-trial-credit)

When registering a new account on Exoscale, you might be entitled to get free credit to try the platform.

In order to get the credit, we ask you to save your credit card during the registration process, by choosing to enter our platform in [Post-paid Billing mode](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#billing-modes)
.

When saving your credit card, Exoscale will reserve an amount of 20 EUR/CHF (depending on your currency) on it. The card will not be actually charged, the amount is only _pre-authorized_ and will be released by your credit card provider depending on its own rules (usually 30 days or the end of the month).

Upon successful registration, your account will be immediately credited 20 EUR/CHF (depending on your currency), and you can try our services for free.

By saving your credit card, your account will be configured in [Post-paid Billing](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#billing-modes)
. Would you consume more than the free allowance, your credit card will be charged accordingly. Otherwise, nothing is due on your side, and you can decide at any moment to leave or close your account with no costs.

Exoscale reserves the right to not provide the free credit for multiple registrations, and to expire the offered credit.

Operating on the platform[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/quick-start/#operating-on-the-platform)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Once successfully registered, you can start using all Exoscale product and services. You can find detailed documentation about each service on [community.exoscale.com](https://community.exoscale.com/)
.

You will be billed only for what you will actually use, and you can leave the platform at any time.

[You can find more information about billing and invoicing here](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/)
.

> **NOTE**  
> At any point in time, would you need further support please contact us via [support ticket](https://portal.exoscale.com/tickets)
>  (existing customers only) or [contact form](https://www.exoscale.com/contact/)
> .

# Organization – Exoscale Documentation

Organization
============

Any action you perform on the Exoscale platform is done in the context of an Organization.

Organizations represent a workspace for several users, such as a company or a team within a company. Each Organization has their own billing address, credit, users, and resources. You can create multiple Organizations to fit your own business model, isolating resources by project, team, or any other criteria that might suit you.

When you create a new organization in addition to the ones you are already a member of, the new organization starts in a suspended state. You will need to register payment details and add funds before you can create resources.

Organizations can have unlimited users, and have no additional cost beyond the resources they use.

You can switch context between organizations by clicking on the current Organization name in the upper right of the Portal.

Create an Organization[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/organization/#create-an-organization)

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

From the top right dropdown menu in the web portal you can access the “Organization Management” view, from which you can:

*   see the detailed list of Organizations you belong to
*   switch context to any Organization you belong to
*   create a new Organization
*   leave any Organization you are member of, as long as you are not its sole Owner.

Upon creation, your organization is immediately available, but appears in a `suspended` state. You will need to add credit to it, or setup an adapted Billing Mode [from the web portal](https://portal.exoscale.com/organization/billing/mode)
, under `Organization` > `Billing` > `Billing Mode`.

Switching Context[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/organization/#switching-context)

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

To switch context between Organizations, you can as well select the name of the desired Organization in the top right dropdown menu of the Portal.

Add and Manage Users in your Organization[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/organization/#add-and-manage-users-in-your-organization)

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

In order to add other users to your workspace for them to collaborate with you in the web portal, you need to set up IAM Roles defining their level of access, then invite them to join your Organization.

Please [refer to the IAM section to learn more](https://community.exoscale.com/product/iam/)
.

Working with Organizations[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/organization/#working-with-organizations)

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Organizations can be structured in 2 different ways:

*   Independent
*   As a tree

### Independent Organization[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/organization/#independent-organization)

Independent organizations are not linked to each other. They can be used to create isolated environments, such as development, staging, or production environments. Each organization has its own set of resources and billing information.

### Tree Organization[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/organization/#tree-organization)

Tree organizations are linked to each other. They can be used to create a hierarchical structure, such as a company with multiple departments or a university with multiple faculties. With tree organizations, you can define a parent-child relationship between organizations, allowing you to separate accesses and permissions at each level. Only the top-level organization receives billing information. Each child organization receives usage information for resources allocated to it.

Tree organizations can be nested up to 5 levels, allowing you to create complex organizational structures. Tree organizations must be created by an organization owner and this owner must be owner of all child organizations.

To create a tree organization, reach out to our support team.

# Two-Factor Authentication – Exoscale Documentation

Two-Factor Authentication
=========================

Two-factor authentication provides the best possible protection for your account.

With two-factor authentication enabled, you enter your password and a secure code generated by your mobile device. Stealing your password is not enough for an attacker to gain access to your Exoscale account.

This method combines the security of something you know (your password) and something you have (your mobile device that generates secure codes).

Set up[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/two-factor-auth/#set-up)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------

From the top `Account` dropdown menu, go to `Account Details` and then click on the `Password and Security` tab.

*   click on `Set up two-factor verification`.
*   Enter your account password
*   Scan the QR code with your device’s authenticator app, or enter the code in the app
*   Your app will give you another code to enter in the form - enter the code and click on submit
*   You will be presented with a confirmation window and a set of backup codes
*   Make sure to **save your backup codes**

If you do not have an authenticator app yet, here are a few possibilities:

*   [FreeOTP](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.fedorahosted.freeotp)
     for Android.
*   [Duo Mobile](https://guide.duo.com/third-party-accounts)
     for Android and iOS.
*   [OATH Toolkit](https://github.com/jessiewy/oath-toolkit)
     (with `oathtool --totp -b yoursecret`) for Linux/BSD/macOS .

Implications[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/two-factor-auth/#implications)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

> **NOTE**  
> When you have two-factor authentication enabled, it is **vitally important** to remember: (1) to keep your authenticator app up to date (2) to safely store of your TOTP secret (3) to the safely store your backup codes.

If your authenticator app is lost, you can access your account and reset your two-factor authentication with the backup codes we provide on the initial setup.

If you have no backup codes and you are locked out of your account, you can contact our Support team **as a last resort**, but our ability to assist you might be limited.

At Exoscale we take your account security **extremely seriously**. We will need to authenticate your identity with a high degree of confidence. Please note that you might have to provide additional identification.

Please **load your public SSH key** to your account.

If an RSA public key is registered in your private account, we will send you the digest of a challenge, which you can sign with your private key. We can use the public key registered on your account to verify the signed digest and reset the account.

Please **save a phone number** on your account.

If a phone number is registered on the account, we will send a challenge to the phone number and act on the request if the correct challenge is sent back to us in the account reset request.

If those methods are unavailable or fail, there will be no action on our side. Unfortunately, you will be not able to access your account.

# Single-Sign-On – Exoscale Documentation

Single-Sign-On
==============

Allow single sign-on for users on an Exoscale account using an Open ID Connect, also known as OIDC. identity provider. SSO organization connect for Exoscale is available to every organizations.

Activation[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/#activation)

---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Accessible to users with **OWNER** user role in the **IAM** section of the main navigation, under **SSO**.

![](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/sso-activation.png "SSO activation")

SSO activation

Configuration[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/#configuration)

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Accessible to users with **OWNER** user role in the **IAM** section of the main navigation, under **SSO**.

![](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/sso-configuration.png "SSO configuration")

SSO configuration

You need to enter:

*   your OIDC `Client ID`
*   the OIDC `Issuer URL`
*   your OIDC `Client Secret`

This is the URL you need to authorize on the OIDC provider side: `https://portal.exoscale.com/sso-authenticate/<sso-org-name>`

![](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/sso-shareable.png "SSO shareable endpoints")

SSO shareable endpoints

Login[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/#login)

-----------------------------------------------------------------

The URL for the SSO login page: `https://portal.exoscale.com/sso-login/<sso-org-name>`

![](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/sso-login.png "SSO login")

SSO login

When a user logs in an organization using SSO, a new, unique user is created inside this organization with the **TECH** role.

The username is a universally unique identifier (or UUID) to allow for a standard non-SSO user with the same email address.

Authorization[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/#authorization)

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------

You can authorize in two ways:

*   Use an ID token introspection with a [Common Expression Language](https://github.com/google/cel-spec)
     (or CEL) query.
*   Set up your own proxy with a [DEX](https://dexidp.io/)
     server or equivalent.

### Authorization with ID token introspection[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/#authorization-with-id-token-introspection)

Enter two values to perform ID token payload introspection to authorize a user:

*   `OIDC Additional Claims` which contains claim names (space separated)
*   a `Common Expression Language Authorization Expression` which is evaluated at login time against the ID token payload.

![](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/sso-authorization.png "SSO authorization")

SSO authorization

#### OIDC additional claims[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/#oidc-additional-claims)

The standard claims which are always part of the ID token payload are:

*   `openid`
*   `profile`
*   `email`

If you need to introspect additional claims, you need to enter them in the `OIDC Additional Claims` field.

#### Common Expression Language Authorization Expression[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/#common-expression-language-authorization-expression)

This Common Expression Language query will be evaluated at login time against the ID token payload.

The CEL authorization expression must return the UUID of the desired IAM Role for the user.

    email.endsWith('@exoscale.com') ? 'dc097e90-4636-4d2a-a629-670ab4395209' : false

The role of the SSO users will be updated accordingly over time with the return value of the CEL expression. This means that by updating the IAM Role UUID in the above example, the user would be assigned to it.

Any return value that is not a valid IAM Role UUID will deny access to the platform.

![](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/sso-login-fail.png "SSO Login failed")

SSO Login failed

#### Removal of SSO users[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/#removal-of-sso-users)

Every time a new SSO user is recognized by Exoscale, a new special account is created on the platform. You can see all of your Organization’s users in the user management view in the web portal: `IAM` > `Users`.

In order to fully remove a SSO user, it is not enough to remove it from your Exoscale Organization, you will also need to prevent his ability to login via the OIDC provider, or the user will simply be recreated at its next login.

##### Common Expression Language resources[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/sso-auth/#common-expression-language-resources)

*   [Introduction](https://github.com/google/cel-spec/blob/master/doc/intro.md)
    
*   [Language reference](https://github.com/google/cel-spec/blob/master/doc/langdef.md)

# Billing – Exoscale Documentation

Billing
=======

Each resource, unless noted otherwise, is billed by the exact time or unit of metering you consume. Your credit status is updated every hour. Most resources are metered on exact consumption usage or up to the minute.

Exoscale supports CHF, EUR or USD currencies.

We accept Visa, Mastercard and American Express cards supporting 3D Secure payment verification. We also accept PayPal (Pre-Paid Mode only).

Billing Modes[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#billing-modes)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following billing modes are available:

*   Pre-paid billing with credit card or PayPal
*   Post-paid billing with credit card
*   Post-paid billing with wire transfer

### Pre-paid Billing[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#pre-paid-billing)

You add funds to your organization **before** consuming resources. When your organization balance reaches zero, the organization is suspended. Make sure to keep the credit in your account topped up to avoid suspension.

### Post-paid Billing with Credit Card[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#post-paid-billing-with-credit-card)

You register one ore more credit cards and consume resources freely. Every month, we automatically charge your credit card marked **default** for the amount you have consumed.

### Post-paid Billing with Wire Transfer[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#post-paid-billing-with-wire-transfer)

Established companies with a proved line of credit can request access to post-paid billing with wire transfer after a manual vetting process. Every month, we will issue an invoice to your company.

### Invoicing[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#invoicing)

For Pre-paid Billing mode invoices are sent via email immediately after the payment has been successfully processed.

For Post-Paid Billing mode, both with Credit Card and Wire Transfer, invoices are sent monthly - the 1st of the month, for the past month.

In Post-paid Billing with Credit Card, invoices are sent 3 days prior to the withdrawal of the invoiced amount from your credit card. Before being billed under the standard monthly cycle, a newly created organization will be invoiced regularly until it reaches the “trusted” status. This occurs either after a set period following the first payment or once usage reaches 160 CHF/EUR/USD.

Please note, that Wire invoices are NOT automatically charged from your bank account. You will need to manually pay Wire invoices within 10 days after invoice date.

### Change Billing Mode[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#change-billing-mode)

If you are in **pre-paid billing mode**, you can move to post-paid by credit card mode by registering a credit card in the Credit Card Management view. For post-paid by wire transfer please open a support ticket.

If you are in **any post-paid billing mode** (card or wire transfer), you can move to any other mode by contacting our support team.

### Low Credit Notification[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#low-credit-notification)

Pre-paid accounts have the ability to [set a threshold](https://portal.exoscale.com/organization/billing)
 at which they will be alerted about their credit being too low, in order to be able to recharge before being suspended. The default is set to 15 CHF/EUR/USD (depending on your currency).

### Invoice Delivery[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#invoice-delivery)

Invoices are sent via email to all users with a default IAM Role of type Owner or Billing.

In addition, you can add an extra email address within an organization that does not need to be a user to receive invoices. This can be done directly from the [Billing Details](https://portal.exoscale.com/organization/billing)
 of your organization.

Credit Cards[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#credit-cards)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Credit card details are safely stored by our payment processing partner: Adyen. Exoscale does not store credit card details.

You can manage your credit cards and add a new one in the Credit Card Management view.

When adding a new credit card, remember to set it as default in your credit card list if you want it to be your default payment method.

Products Billing[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#products-billing)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Compute[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/#compute)

--------------------------------------------------------------------

Exoscale Compute pricing is split between instance pricing and disk pricing. This allows you to mix and match CPU, RAM and disk profiles to get the best value.

We do not charge you for:

*   inbound traffic
*   support for data portability or reversibility

**Billing of powered off machines**

Powered off machines will only be charged for resources still in use. Therefore, we will continue to charge your account for the volume attached to your instance. During the trial period, computing resources are charged even when the machines are stopped. This behavior is converted to the default behavior on first payment.

**Billing of network usage**

The bandwidth is billed hourly and takes the number of machines running during the last one-hour period.

There are 1.42 GB of output bandwidth for free per machine. If a machine does not use its free tier, it is reported on other running machines of the same organization. So, with 5 machines running during the last hour, there are 7.1 GB of free output bandwidth. The exceeding bandwidth is billed.

![](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/billing/picto-bandwidth-faq.svg "Bandwidth Billing")

Bandwidth Billing

**When credit runs out**

If your billing method is Pre-paid and your credit goes below the threshold you have set (15 CHF/EUR/USD by default), you receive an email notification. If you don’t add credit and your credit runs out, your machines will be stopped and your account will be suspended. You will not be able to start your machines until you add credit to your funds.

# Data Center Zones – Exoscale Documentation

Data Center Zones
=================

Exoscale uses zones to geographically distinguish locations. An Exoscale zone is:

*   identified by a country code: CH, DE,…
*   hosted in a physical datacenter facility
*   each facility can be comprised of several rooms or buildings

Building around the zone concept[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/dc-zones/#building-around-the-zone-concept)

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

At Exoscale, zones are independant from one another. This means a failure in a specific zone and service has no impact on the other zones.

Most items are attached to a single zone only: a compute instance, an object storage bucket, private network, NLB, EIP,… Only a few items span globally: API key and security group

# Shared Responsibility Model – Exoscale Documentation

Shared Responsibility Model
===========================

Overview[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/compliance/shared-responsability-model/#overview)

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Security and compliance in the cloud are a **shared responsibility** between **Exoscale** and its **customers**.  
While Exoscale ensures the security **of the cloud**—the infrastructure, platform services, and managed components—customers are responsible for the security **in the cloud**, meaning how they configure, deploy, and manage their own environments and data.

The objective of this model is to provide clarity about which party is accountable for which controls, ensuring that all security and compliance requirements are properly addressed.

This model applies to:

*   **IaaS services** (Compute, Object Storage, Private Networks, etc.)
*   **DBaaS services** (managed PostgreSQL, MySQL, Kafka, Valkey, etc.)

1\. Responsibility Split[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/compliance/shared-responsability-model/#1-responsibility-split)

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Exoscale Responsibilities[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/compliance/shared-responsability-model/#exoscale-responsibilities)

Exoscale is responsible for securing and maintaining the **underlying infrastructure**, including:

*   **Physical security** of data centers and network infrastructure
*   **Virtualization and orchestration layers** used to provision resources
*   **Platform-level identity and access management**, including console and API access
*   **Core service availability**, redundancy, and disaster recovery
*   **Encryption of data at rest** in managed layers (e.g., Object Storage, DBaaS volumes)
*   **Vulnerability management and patching** of the platform and managed services
*   **Monitoring and logging** of the platform environment for operational and security events
*   **Regulatory compliance** of the infrastructure (ISO 27001, SOC 2, C5, HDS, etc.)

### Customer Responsibilities[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/compliance/shared-responsability-model/#customer-responsibilities)

Customers are responsible for securing and managing the **resources they control**, such as:

*   **Configuration and operation** of virtual machines, containers, and applications
*   **Classification, protection, and lawful processing** of their data
*   **Implementation of encryption** within applications and databases (including field-level or client-side)
*   **Network design and access controls** within their Virtual Private Networks.
*   **User and role management** within their tenant and workloads
*   **Logging and monitoring** of their applications, guest OS, and user activities
*   **Backup and restoration** of their data (IaaS)
*   **Compliance with applicable legal and regulatory requirements**, such as GDPR or sector-specific laws

2\. Managed Service (DBaaS) Context[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/compliance/shared-responsability-model/#2-managed-service-dbaas-context)

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

For managed services like **Exoscale DBaaS**, Exoscale operates part of the service in partnership with **Aiven**, an ISO 27001-certified provider.  
Exoscale and Aiven are jointly responsible for the **security of the managed service platform**, while customers remain responsible for **how they use the service**—including schema design, data protection, and access control within their databases.

All DBaaS instances are hosted **within Exoscale infrastructure** and **within the EEA or Switzerland**, in line with Exoscale’s data residency commitments.

3\. Shared Responsibility Matrix (SRM)[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/compliance/shared-responsability-model/#3-shared-responsibility-matrix-srm)

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following matrix summarizes the division of responsibilities across control domains for both IaaS and DBaaS (PaaS) environments.

| Control Domain | IaaS (Customer) | IaaS (Exoscale) | PaaS/DBaaS (Customer) | PaaS/DBaaS (Exoscale/Aiven) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Data classification & app-layer access | **Customer** | –   | **Customer** | –   |
| Encryption **in transit** | **Customer** (workloads) | **Exoscale** (platform) | **Customer** (client→service) | **Exoscale/Aiven** (service endpoints) |
| Encryption **at rest** | **Customer** (VM guest/app layer; CMKs where supported) | **Exoscale** (object storage layer only) | **Customer** (app schema/field-level) | **Exoscale/Aiven** (volumes and backup) |
| Key management | **Customer** (CMK/HYOK where available) | **Exoscale** (platform keys, object storage encryption key) | **Customer** | **Aiven/Exoscale** (service keys; CMK options per service) |
| IAM within tenant | **Customer** | –   | **Customer** | –   |
| Platform IAM, SSO for console/API | –   | **Exoscale** | –   | **Exoscale** |
| Network security (PrivNet, SGs, WAF in-tenant) | **Customer** | –   | **Customer** | –   |
| Perimeter/DC physical security | –   | **Exoscale/Providers** | –   | **Exoscale/Providers** |
| Vulnerability mgmt (guest/containers) | **Customer** | –   | **Customer** | –   |
| Vulnerability mgmt (platform/services) | –   | **Exoscale** | –   | **Exoscale/Aiven** |
| Logging/monitoring (tenant workloads) | **Customer** | –   | **Customer** | –   |
| Platform/service logs & monitoring | –   | **Exoscale** | –   | **Exoscale/Aiven** |
| Backup/restore (tenant data) | **Customer** (IaaS) | –   | **Customer** config | **Exoscale/Aiven** (managed backup per service) |
| BC/DR objectives (platform) | –   | **Exoscale** | –   | **Exoscale/Aiven** |

4\. Practical Implications[](https://community.exoscale.com/platform/compliance/shared-responsability-model/#4-practical-implications)

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

*   **Customers retain full control** of their hosted data and workloads.
*   Exoscale provides the **secure, compliant infrastructure** and ensures **operational resilience** of the underlying platform.
*   In regulated contexts (e.g. healthcare, financial services), customers can use this matrix to **demonstrate their shared compliance posture**.
*   The model supports frameworks such as **ISO 27001**, **HDS**, **SOC 2**, **C5**, and **NIS2** by clarifying which controls fall within each party’s domain.

At **Exoscale**, we recognize that health data is among the most sensitive categories of information our customers may process. We therefore apply **the highest standards of confidentiality, integrity, and availability** to the infrastructure and services that host such data.

    *   Security controls are aligned with **ISO/IEC 27001**, **SOC 2 Type II**, and **HDS** standards.
    *   All data is **encrypted in transit (TLS)** and **at rest**, with **key management under Exoscale or customer control**.

    *   Customers retain control over their virtual machines, applications, and encryption keys.
    *   Exoscale provides a transparent **Shared Responsibility Matrix** in its DPA and HDS appendices.
    *   Detailed **subprocessor information** (e.g. Equinix, A1 Telekom Austria, Aiven Oy) is publicly available.
4.  **Incident Response and Breach Notification**
    
    *   Security incidents impacting health data are managed under our **centralized Security Operations process**.
    *   Notifications are made without undue delay in accordance with **GDPR Articles 33–34**, **HDS requirements**, or **HIPAA 45 CFR 164.410**, as applicable.
5.  **Auditability and Continuous Assurance**
    
    *   Exoscale maintains third-party **audit reports** (HDS, ISO/IEC 27001, SOC 2 Type 2, C5 Type 2).
    *   Customers may request summaries or perform audits under the terms of the **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** or **HDS Addendum**.

# Billing – Exoscale Documentation

Billing
=======

Each resource, unless noted otherwise, is billed by the exact time or unit of metering you consume. Your credit status is updated every hour. Most resources are metered on exact consumption usage or up to the minute.

Exoscale supports CHF, EUR or USD currencies.

We accept Visa, Mastercard and American Express cards supporting 3D Secure payment verification. We also accept PayPal (Pre-Paid Mode only).

Billing Modes[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#billing-modes)

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following billing modes are available:

*   Pre-paid billing with credit card or PayPal
*   Post-paid billing with credit card
*   Post-paid billing with wire transfer

### Pre-paid Billing[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#pre-paid-billing)

You add funds to your organization **before** consuming resources. When your organization balance reaches zero, the organization is suspended. Make sure to keep the credit in your account topped up to avoid suspension.

### Post-paid Billing with Credit Card[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#post-paid-billing-with-credit-card)

You register one ore more credit cards and consume resources freely. Every month, we automatically charge your credit card marked **default** for the amount you have consumed.

### Post-paid Billing with Wire Transfer[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#post-paid-billing-with-wire-transfer)

Established companies with a proved line of credit can request access to post-paid billing with wire transfer after a manual vetting process. Every month, we will issue an invoice to your company.

### Invoicing[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#invoicing)

For Pre-paid Billing mode invoices are sent via email immediately after the payment has been successfully processed.

For Post-Paid Billing mode, both with Credit Card and Wire Transfer, invoices are sent monthly - the 1st of the month, for the past month.

In Post-paid Billing with Credit Card, invoices are sent 3 days prior to the withdrawal of the invoiced amount from your credit card. Before being billed under the standard monthly cycle, a newly created organization will be invoiced regularly until it reaches the “trusted” status. This occurs either after a set period following the first payment or once usage reaches 160 CHF/EUR/USD.

Please note, that Wire invoices are NOT automatically charged from your bank account. You will need to manually pay Wire invoices within 10 days after invoice date.

### Change Billing Mode[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#change-billing-mode)

If you are in **pre-paid billing mode**, you can move to post-paid by credit card mode by registering a credit card in the Credit Card Management view. For post-paid by wire transfer please open a support ticket.

If you are in **any post-paid billing mode** (card or wire transfer), you can move to any other mode by contacting our support team.

### Low Credit Notification[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#low-credit-notification)

Pre-paid accounts have the ability to [set a threshold](https://portal.exoscale.com/organization/billing)
 at which they will be alerted about their credit being too low, in order to be able to recharge before being suspended. The default is set to 15 CHF/EUR/USD (depending on your currency).

### Invoice Delivery[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#invoice-delivery)

Invoices are sent via email to all users with a default IAM Role of type Owner or Billing.

In addition, you can add an extra email address within an organization that does not need to be a user to receive invoices. This can be done directly from the [Billing Details](https://portal.exoscale.com/organization/billing)
 of your organization.

Credit Cards[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#credit-cards)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Credit card details are safely stored by our payment processing partner: Adyen. Exoscale does not store credit card details.

You can manage your credit cards and add a new one in the Credit Card Management view.

When adding a new credit card, remember to set it as default in your credit card list if you want it to be your default payment method.

Products Billing[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#products-billing)

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Compute[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#compute)

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Exoscale Compute pricing is split between instance pricing and disk pricing. This allows you to mix and match CPU, RAM and disk profiles to get the best value.

We do not charge you for:

*   inbound traffic
*   support for data portability or reversibility

**Billing of powered off machines**

Powered off machines will only be charged for resources still in use. Therefore, we will continue to charge your account for the volume attached to your instance. During the trial period, computing resources are charged even when the machines are stopped. This behavior is converted to the default behavior on first payment.

**Billing of network usage**

The bandwidth is billed hourly and takes the number of machines running during the last one-hour period.

There are 1.42 GB of output bandwidth for free per machine. If a machine does not use its free tier, it is reported on other running machines of the same organization. So, with 5 machines running during the last hour, there are 7.1 GB of free output bandwidth. The exceeding bandwidth is billed.

![](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/picto-bandwidth-faq.svg "Bandwidth Billing")

Bandwidth Billing

**When credit runs out**

If your billing method is Pre-paid and your credit goes below the threshold you have set (15 CHF/EUR/USD by default), you receive an email notification. If you don’t add credit and your credit runs out, your machines will be stopped and your account will be suspended. You will not be able to start your machines until you add credit to your funds.

While your account is suspended, negative credit will continue to add up, as you are charged for the volume attached to your instance.

When you add credit, you need to add enough to have a positive account balance to start your instance again.

If you choose to not add credit, all your instances will be removed after several weeks and reminders.

Storage[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#storage)

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Exoscale Storage is charged based on tiers.

Pricing of the tiers is cumulative. The first 512 TiB are charged at Tier 1 level, 512 TiB to 1 PiB at Tier 2 level, everything above at Tier 3 level.

*   0.0198 CHF/EUR/USD per GiB.month stored (e.g. for Tier 1 level)
*   0.0200 CHF/EUR/USD per GiB of networking out

There is no other charge.

So if you store a 1 GB file it will only cost you 0.0198 CHF/EUR/USD. Retrieving the file will cost you 0.0200 CHF/EUR/USD.

DNS[](https://community.exoscale.com/documentation/platform/billing/#dns)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------

Exoscale DNS has three available subscriptions:

*   **Small**: 1 CHF/EUR/USD per month for 1 DNS zone
*   **Medium**: 5 CHF/EUR/USD per month for 10 DNS zones
*   **Large**: 25 CHF/EUR/USD per month for 50 DNS zones

You are billed at monthly intervals. You can upgrade and downgrade your subscription at any time, and you will be charged in proportion to your usage.

As an example, if you subscribe to a DNS plan for 10 days and then unsubscribe, you will be charged 10/30th of the price.
