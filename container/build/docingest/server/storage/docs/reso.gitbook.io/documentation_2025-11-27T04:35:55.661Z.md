# The Problem | mytoast

![Page cover](https://reso.gitbook.io/mytoast/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F671579982-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FdEvqPChDSFzgVUtQQdEb%252Fuploads%252F10ZiGov3edn1rOseVXCN%252FWhatsApp%2520Image%25202025-06-27%2520at%252013.36.18.jpeg%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D87d3599e-8c5a-42e5-b4f9-16b0d10fab6b&width=1248&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=b680dbbd&sv=2)

Let’s take a step back and really look at the space.

Token launches today feel more like a psychological game than a product launch.

Everywhere you turn, there’s pressure — “Launch now, earn fees, share revenue, be the next big creator.” But what’s really happening?

Most platforms use fee-sharing models that sound rewarding, but in reality, they push traders into unhealthy behavior. Buy fast, sell faster, get stuck holding — not because the token has value, but because the system is designed to trap you into thinking it does.

At the same time, people are pushed to launch tokens they don’t even believe in — just to chase rewards.

It’s not sustainable. It’s not healthy. And it’s definitely not fair.

A launch should be the beginning of something real — not a cycle of fear, greed, and short-term exit plans.

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/mytoast/introduction/the-problem#how-mytoast-fixes-it)

How mytoast Fixes It?


------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

We built mytoast to bring things back to what actually matters — real launches, real communities, and real growth.

And yes — we know. Flat price launches don’t create FOMO. They don’t spark wild speculation. They’re not “sexy” in the way most degens expect.

But that’s exactly the point.

Flat price is the cleanest way to launch — no tricks, no traps, no whales playing games.

It gives space for something deeper: true collaboration between creators and communities. No pressure. No manipulation. Just a fair start, and a clear path forward.

**On mytoast:** • Tokens launch instantly at a fixed, honest price. • Creators don’t get rewarded just for showing up. • They earn after their token proves it can survive — hitting volume, holders, and activity. • Once that milestone is reached, the token becomes Fully Toasted, and real rewards are unlocked.

This model doesn’t magically fix everything — but it sets the foundation.

Think of it like preparing for battle at the base. Everyone starts equal. And only after the mission goal is reached — that’s when the real action begins. That’s when trenches form. That’s when strategy kicks in. That’s when both creator and community move together.

Because real tokens aren’t built by FOMO. They’re built by alignment, patience, and the kind of heat that toasts something worth serving.

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/features-and-functionalities/staking#overview-of-staking)

**Overview of Staking**

*   **Purpose** Staking on Rectoverso allows users to lock their tokens in programs created by developers. These programs aim to increase user engagement and provide rewards for token holders.
    
*   **Variety** Rectoverso offers multiple staking options to suit different user needs and preferences.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/features-and-functionalities/staking#staking-programs)

**Staking Programs**

*   **Developer-Created Programs** Developers can create and manage their own staking programs to promote their tokens. These programs may offer unique incentives and benefits.
    
*   **Fixed-Term Staking** Users commit their tokens for a specific period, earning higher rewards for longer commitments. Ideal for users seeking predictable returns.
    
*   **Flexible Staking** Users can stake and unstake their tokens at any time, offering flexibility. Rewards may vary based on the staking period and program terms.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/features-and-functionalities/staking#earn-rewards)

**Earn Rewards**

*   **Mechanism** Rewards are based on terms set by developers and can include additional tokens, exclusive features, or other incentives.
    
*   **Distribution** Rewards are typically distributed at regular intervals (daily, weekly, or monthly), as specified by the staking program.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/features-and-functionalities/staking#community-engagement)

**Community Engagement:**

*   **Incentive Programs** Special programs that offer bonuses for long-term staking or extra benefits for active participation, fostering a strong community.
    
*   **Developer Interaction** Staking programs facilitate closer interaction between developers and their communities, enhancing engagement and support.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/features-and-functionalities/staking#staking-rewards)

**Staking Rewards:**

*   **Calculation** Rewards are calculated based on factors like the amount staked, duration, and specific conditions set by the program.
    
*   **Tracking** Users can track their rewards and performance through tools and dashboards provided on the Rectoverso platform.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/features-and-functionalities/staking#unstaking-process)

**Unstaking Process:**

# Understanding Staking and Rewards | Rectoverso (RESO)

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/informational/understanding-staking-and-rewards#key-concepts)

**Key Concepts**

*   **Promoter:** The entity that creates a staking offer.
    
*   **Staker:** A user who deposits tokens to participate in the staking offer.
    
*   **Epoch:** A unit of time used in the system.
    
*   **Staking Offer:** A proposal created by a promoter specifying the details of the staking opportunity, like the duration, reward amount, minimum and maximum deposit, and the type of token to be staked.
    
*   **Staking Period:** The duration of the staking offer, defined by a start epoch and an end epoch.
    
*   **Reward Amount:** The total amount of tokens the promoter offers as rewards for the entire staking period.
    
*   **Shares:** Represents the portion of the total rewards that a staker is entitled to, calculated based on their deposit amount and the timing of their deposits and withdrawals.
    
*   **Claimable Reward:** The amount of reward a staker can withdraw at any given time.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/informational/understanding-staking-and-rewards#how-staking-works)

How Staking Works

1.  **Offer Creation:** A promoter creates a staking offer, specifying the staking period (start and end epochs), the reward amount each epoch, the token to be staked, and other parameters like minimum and maximum deposit limits.
    
2.  **Staking:** Users (stakers) can deposit tokens into the staking offer during the staking period, but can stake before the staking period.
    
3.  **Reward Calculation:** The system calculates each staker's share of the reward based on their deposit amount and the timing of their deposits and withdrawals.
    
4.  **Claiming Rewards:** Stakers can claim their accumulated rewards after the staking period ends or during the staking period if they unstake the token.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/informational/understanding-staking-and-rewards#reward-calculation-details)

Reward Calculation Details

The reward calculation is based on the concept of "shares." Here's how it works:

1.  **Share Calculation :**
    
    *   Initial Shares: When a staker deposits tokens, they receive an initial number of shares proportional to their deposit amount and the time remaining until the end of the staking period, but the share will be counted after the staking period has started.
        
    *   Share Adjustment: Shares are adjusted at the end of each epoch based on the staker's deposit amount and the total deposit amount at that time.
        
    
2.  **Reward per Epoch Calculation :**
    
    *   Each epoch within the staking period has a designated reward amount, as defined in the staking offer. To determine a staker's reward for a specific epoch, the program looks at the proportion of their shares relative to the total shares held by all stakers in that epoch.
        
    *   Essentially, the reward for an epoch is divided among all stakers based on their share "weight" at that point in time. A staker with a larger share of the total naturally receives a larger portion of the epoch's reward.
        
    *   The calculation can be visualized as follows : '`_Staker Reward for Epoch = (Staker's Shares in Epoch / Total Shares of All Stakers in Epoch) * Reward Amount for Epoch'_`
        
    *   **Example:** If a staker holds 100 shares in epoch 775, and the total shares of all stakers in that same epoch is 1000, and if epoch 775 has a reward amount of 200 RESO, then the staker would receive (100 / 1000) \* 200 RESO = 20 RESO for that epoch.
        
    *   This ensures that rewards are distributed in direct proportion to each staker's contribution, as measured by their share of the total stake.
        
    
3.  **Cumulative Reward :**
    
    *   A staker's cumulative reward is the sum of their rewards earned in each epoch.
        
    
4.  **Claimable Reward Calculation :** _'_`_Claimable Reward = Total Reward Earned - Total Reward Claimed'_`
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/informational/understanding-staking-and-rewards#example-scenario)

Example Scenario

Let's say a promoter create an staking offer from 770th epoch to 780th epoch, with reward of 200 RESO each epoch. The following events occur :

*   At 770th epoch, Staker 1 deposit stake of value 50 RESO

*   At 772th epoch, Staker 1 unstake 25 RESO
    
*   At 773th epoch, Staker 2 deposit stake of value 50 RESO

    import { createMytoastProgramClient, prepareLpadFundInstructions } from '@mytoast/sdk';
    import { Connection, Keypair, PublicKey, Transaction, LAMPORTS_PER_SOL } from '@solana/web3.js';
    import { TOKEN_PROGRAM_ID } from '@solana/spl-token';
    import fs from 'fs';
    
    // 1. Setup connection and client
    const NETWORK = 'mainnet-beta'; // or 'devnet' for testing
    const RPC_URL = `https://api.${NETWORK}.solana.com`;
    const connection = new Connection(RPC_URL, 'confirmed');
    const client = createMytoastProgramClient(connection, { apiUrl: 'https://api.mytoast.io' });
    
    // 2. Load user wallet
    const walletData = JSON.parse(fs.readFileSync('./wallet.json', 'utf8'));
    const payer = Keypair.fromSecretKey(new Uint8Array(walletData));
    
    // 3. Set launchpad and token info
    const lpadPda = new PublicKey('...'); // Replace with your launchpad PDA
    const offeredTokenMint = new PublicKey('...'); // Replace with launchpad token mint
    const fundingAmount = 1 * LAMPORTS_PER_SOL; // 1 SOL
    
    // 4. Prepare fund instruction
    const { lpadFundInstruction } = prepareLpadFundInstructions(client, {
      payer: payer.publicKey,
      benefactor: payer.publicKey,
      lpadPda,
      fundingAmount,
      offeredTokenMint,
      offeredTokenProgram: TOKEN_PROGRAM_ID,
    });
    
    // 5. Create and send transaction
    const tx = new Transaction().add(lpadFundInstruction);
    try {
      const signature = await connection.sendTransaction(tx, [payer]);
      console.log('Funded launchpad! TX:', signature);
    } catch (error) {
      console.error('Failed to fund launchpad:', error.message);
    }

    const { lpadRefundInstruction } = prepareLpadRefundInstructions(client, {
      benefactor: benefactor.publicKey,
      offeredTokenMint: tokenMint,
      offeredTokenProgram: TOKEN_PROGRAM_ID,
      lpadPda,
      payer: benefactor.publicKey,
      refundedQuantity,
    });
    
    // 6. Create and send transaction
    const tx = new Transaction().add(lpadRefundInstruction);
    try {
      const signature = await connection.sendTransaction(tx, [benefactor]);
      console.log('Refunded launchpad! TX:', signature);
    } catch (error) {
      console.error('Failed to refund launchpad:', error.message);
    }

*   Use the `findLpadPda` helper to derive the launchpad PDA if not already known

# Launchpad Creation | mytoast

This comprehensive guide explains how developers can create and publish launchpads on the Mytoast platform using the `createMytoastLaunchpad` function from the Mytoast SDK.

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/mytoast/developers/integration/launchpad-creation#overview)

Overview

The Mytoast SDK provides a simple interface for creating token launchpads on Solana. The `createMytoastLaunchpad` function handles the complete process of:

1.  **Token Creation** - Mints a new SPL token with your specifications
    
2.  **Metadata Upload** - Stores token and launchpad metadata on IPFS
    
3.  **Launchpad Initialization** - Creates the on-chain launchpad program account
    
4.  **Authority Transfer** - Secures the token by removing mint authorities
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/mytoast/developers/integration/launchpad-creation#prerequisites)

Prerequisites

Before you begin, ensure you have:

*   **Node.js 18+** environment
    
*   **Solana wallet** with sufficient SOL for transaction fees
    
*   **Token metadata** (name, symbol, description, images)
    
*   **Base64-encoded images** for token icon and launchpad banner
    
*   **Network access** to upload metadata to IPFS
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/mytoast/developers/integration/launchpad-creation#installation)

Installation

Install the required dependencies:

    npm install @mytoast/sdk @solana/web3.js @solana/spl-token

Troubleshooting

#### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/mytoast/developers/integration/launchpad-creation#common-issues)

Common Issues

**"Publisher PDA not found"**

*   Ensure you're connected to the correct network (mainnet/devnet)
    
*   Verify the program is deployed on your chosen network
    

**"Failed to upload metadata"**

*   Check image file sizes (token icon < 1MB, banner < 2MB)
    
*   Ensure images are in PNG or JPEG format
    
*   Verify network connectivity
    

**"Transaction failed"**

*   Ensure wallet has sufficient SOL (~0.01 SOL minimum)
    
*   Check if token symbol is already taken
    
*   Verify all required parameters are provided
    

**"Rate limit exceeded"**

*   Wait a few minutes before retrying
    
*   Consider using a different API endpoint

# Smart Contract Security | Rectoverso (RESO)

Ensuring the security of smart contracts is a critical aspect of the Rectoverso platform.

*   **Formal Verification:** Smart contracts undergo formal verification processes to mathematically prove their correctness and eliminate vulnerabilities.
    
*   **Comprehensive Testing:** All smart contracts are subjected to rigorous testing, including unit tests, integration tests, and penetration tests, to ensure they function as intended and are secure against attacks.
    
*   **Audit by Experts:** Smart contracts are reviewed and audited by blockchain security experts to ensure they meet the highest security standards. Any identified issues are promptly addressed and rectified.
    
*   **Bug Bounty Program:** A bug bounty program is in place to encourage the community and security researchers to report any vulnerabilities in the smart contracts, rewarding them for their contributions to platform security.
