# Token Overview | Rectoverso (RESO)

The RESO token is the native utility token of the Rectoverso platform, designed to facilitate various activities and transactions within the ecosystem. Built on the Solana blockchain, RESO leverages high throughput and low transaction costs, making it ideal for decentralized finance (DeFi) applications. With a fixed total supply of 100 million tokens, RESO aims to provide value to its holders through multiple use cases, supporting both platform utility and community engagement.

RESO is designed to fuel the Rectoverso ecosystem, providing essential functionalities such as paying for transaction fees, participating in staking, accessing the launchpad, earning rewards, and participating in governance. One of the key features of the RESO token is its strategic buyback mechanism, which ensures price stability and potential appreciation.

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/reso-token/token-overview#key-highlights)

**Key Highlights**

*   **Name and Symbol:** The native utility token of the Rectoverso platform is called RESO, with the symbol $RESO.
    
*   **Blockchain Network:** RESO is built on the Solana blockchain, leveraging its high throughput and low transaction costs.
    
*   **Total Supply:** The total supply of RESO tokens is fixed at 100 million, ensuring a predefined limit to prevent inflation.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/reso-token/token-overview#purpose-and-utility)

**Purpose and Utility**

*   **Transaction Fees:** RESO is used to pay for transaction fees on the Rectoverso platform, including trading fees, staking fees, and bridging fees.
    
*   **Staking:** Users can stake RESO tokens to earn rewards, participate in governance, and support network security.
    
*   **Launchpad Access:** Developers pay listing fees in RESO tokens to launch their projects on the Rectoverso Launchpad. Users also use RESO to participate in token sales and other launchpad events.
    
*   **Incentives and Rewards:** RESO tokens are distributed as incentives for liquidity providers, active community members, and participants in various platform activities.
    
*   **Governance Participation:** RESO holders may use their tokens to vote on important proposals, influencing the development and direction of the Rectoverso platform.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/reso-token/token-overview#economic-model)

**Economic Model**

*   **Controlled Inflation:** The tokenomics model is designed to control inflation and provide incentives to token holders, maintaining the token's value over time.
    
*   **Buyback Mechanism:** Highlighting again, the buyback mechanism is a crucial component of RESO's economic model. It involves using 80% of the USD collected from token sales to repurchase RESO tokens at 80% of the distribution price, supporting price stability and potential appreciation.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/reso-token/token-overview#security-and-compliance)

**Security and Compliance**

*   **Audits and Security Measures:** RESO smart contracts undergo regular security audits to ensure their safety and reliability.
    
*   **Regulatory Compliance:** The issuance and use of RESO tokens comply with applicable regulations to protect users and maintain platform integrity.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/reso-token/token-overview#community-and-ecosystem-support)

**Community and Ecosystem Support**

*   **Developer Support:** RESO tokens support ecosystem growth by funding development grants, partnerships, and other community initiatives.
    
*   **User Engagement:** The token fosters active participation in the platform through various incentive programs, ensuring a vibrant and engaged community.

# Understanding Staking and Rewards | Rectoverso (RESO)

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/informational/understanding-staking-and-rewards#key-concepts)

**Key Concepts**

*   **Promoter:** The entity that creates a staking offer.
    
*   **Staker:** A user who deposits tokens to participate in the staking offer.
    
*   **Epoch:** A unit of time used in the system.
    
*   **Staking Offer:** A proposal created by a promoter specifying the details of the staking opportunity, like the duration, reward amount, minimum and maximum deposit, and the type of token to be staked.
    
*   **Staking Period:** The duration of the staking offer, defined by a start epoch and an end epoch.
    
*   **Reward Amount:** The total amount of tokens the promoter offers as rewards for the entire staking period.
    
*   **Shares:** Represents the portion of the total rewards that a staker is entitled to, calculated based on their deposit amount and the timing of their deposits and withdrawals.
    
*   **Claimable Reward:** The amount of reward a staker can withdraw at any given time.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/informational/understanding-staking-and-rewards#how-staking-works)

How Staking Works

1.  **Offer Creation:** A promoter creates a staking offer, specifying the staking period (start and end epochs), the reward amount each epoch, the token to be staked, and other parameters like minimum and maximum deposit limits.
    
2.  **Staking:** Users (stakers) can deposit tokens into the staking offer during the staking period, but can stake before the staking period.
    
3.  **Reward Calculation:** The system calculates each staker's share of the reward based on their deposit amount and the timing of their deposits and withdrawals.
    
4.  **Claiming Rewards:** Stakers can claim their accumulated rewards after the staking period ends or during the staking period if they unstake the token.
    

### 

[](https://reso.gitbook.io/reso-dex/informational/understanding-staking-and-rewards#reward-calculation-details)

Reward Calculation Details

The reward calculation is based on the concept of "shares." Here's how it works:

1.  **Share Calculation :**
    
    *   Initial Shares: When a staker deposits tokens, they receive an initial number of shares proportional to their deposit amount and the time remaining until the end of the staking period, but the share will be counted after the staking period has started.
        
    *   Share Adjustment: Shares are adjusted at the end of each epoch based on the staker's deposit amount and the total deposit amount at that time.
        
    
2.  **Reward per Epoch Calculation :**
    
    *   Each epoch within the staking period has a designated reward amount, as defined in the staking offer. To determine a staker's reward for a specific epoch, the program looks at the proportion of their shares relative to the total shares held by all stakers in that epoch.
        
    *   Essentially, the reward for an epoch is divided among all stakers based on their share "weight" at that point in time. A staker with a larger share of the total naturally receives a larger portion of the epoch's reward.
        
    *   The calculation can be visualized as follows : '`_Staker Reward for Epoch = (Staker's Shares in Epoch / Total Shares of All Stakers in Epoch) * Reward Amount for Epoch'_`
        
    *   **Example:** If a staker holds 100 shares in epoch 775, and the total shares of all stakers in that same epoch is 1000, and if epoch 775 has a reward amount of 200 RESO, then the staker would receive (100 / 1000) \* 200 RESO = 20 RESO for that epoch.
        
    *   This ensures that rewards are distributed in direct proportion to each staker's contribution, as measured by their share of the total stake.
        
    
3.  **Cumulative Reward :**
    
    *   A staker's cumulative reward is the sum of their rewards earned in each epoch.
        
    
4.  **Claimable Reward Calculation :** _'_`_Claimable Reward = Total Reward Earned - Total Reward Claimed'_`
