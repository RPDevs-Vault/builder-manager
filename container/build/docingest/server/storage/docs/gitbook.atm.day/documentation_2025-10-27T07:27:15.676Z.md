All algorithms are built on the following indicators:

### 

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/algo-core#profitable-wallets-activity)

**Profitable Wallets Activity**

Our core indicator — the trigger behind a token appearing in the trending feed. We're gathering wallets with ≈2h+ avg holding time, ≈$10k+ 3d realized PL and ≈60%+ winrate. We label wallets based on on-chain data we’ve collected and assign different “weights” to them.

The list and scoring parameters are continuously updated depending on market conditions and new data we gather.

2

### 

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/algo-core#volume-analytics)

Volume analytics

(Real vs Fake, currently in beta): We track volume spikes and estimate what % of the volume is real vs spoofed by trading bots.

3

### 

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/algo-core#holders-activity)

Holders Activity

We analyze holder dynamics — including holder quality (avg. holding time, past winning performance, etc.)

4

### 

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/algo-core#id-30-other-minor-indicators)

30+ other minor indicators

These include basic signals like DEX paid, dev activity in token, no-mint/no-freeze flags, and more.

All these indicators are dynamic — their weight and target values are regularly adjusted based on current market conditions and our own data about the winning tokens.

On average, we tweak the algorithms 1–2 times per week — either updating existing indicators or introducing new ones.

# Smart Wallets database | atm.day docs

One of the core indicators in our algo is profitable wallets' behaviour. On the Degen tier we give you detailed analytics on those wallets.

**Smart-wallet trading-pattern analysis**

For every profitable wallet we track

*   average holding time
    
*   win rate
    
*   historical entry / exit levels
    
*   some other indicators
    

These metrics let you decide faster whether to take a trade or not, and fine-tune a strategy for each trade.

**Largest, constantly updated copy-trading wallet base**

Each wallet is carefully labeled and appears with every Algo discovery, so you can mirror the ones you trust in one click. It’s the biggest, deeply labeled, regularly updated Solana wallet database.

**Impact:** 70% of traders are 2x more profitable because of these features — based on user feedback and analysis of 200 + customer-shared wallets.

Information (always for past 7d)

How the data looks like

Why it's important

Winrate

49.8%

A trade counts as a “win” when the total SOL sold is greater than the total SOL bought

Median Trade Duration

5h

Shows the trader’s average holding time per token — useful for shaping your own exit strategy

Total trades

Lets you spot the wallet’s overall strategy — and confirms it isn’t running high-frequency trades

# OG Algo Beta | atm.day docs

Our latest — and so far most beloved — algorithm among users. While the original OG Algo triggers new calls every 20 minutes, OG Algo Beta is different: It’s event-based, not time-based.

The core trigger is reaching a specific number of profitable wallets buying a token. That number (X) dynamically adjusts based on current market conditions. This makes OG Beta faster when the market is active — and more selective when it’s not.

OG Algo Beta is available to all ATM Pro users with a 1-month or longer subscription

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/algorithms/og-algo/og-algo-beta#core-sharacteristics)

*   **Discovery timing:** After first strong on-chain traction, before mass awareness
    
*   **Key signal:** Professional Traders’ Activity — entry from historically profitable wallets
    
*   **Use case:** Best fit for consistent 2x setups and short-term plays
    

What's new:

*   **Number of tokens:** around 100 tokens a day (no more 20min timer)
    
*   **Winrate:** 50%+ tokens hit 2x without dipping below -50% first
    
*   **Catches runners earlier** and more often than the current OG Algo

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/algorithms/og-algo/og-algo-beta#best-use-case)

OG Algo Beta is best suited for traders who:

2.  Prefer faster, event-based signals over fixed time intervals
    
3.  Have higher risk tolerance and want early entry potential
    
4.  Are comfortable reacting quickly with a predefined plan

# Trading psychology basics | atm.day docs

You can have the best entry in the world — and still lose money. The problem is rarely the signal. It’s how you handle it. This page covers the core psychological principles that separate consistent traders from emotional ones.

### 

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/trading-psychology-basics#id-1.-formulate-the-exit-strategy-before-you-enter)

1\. Formulate the exit strategy before you enter

Every trade starts with a plan to get out.

Decide before entering:

1.  Target ROI (e.g. 2x, 3x, TRTP)
    
2.  Stop-loss threshold (e.g. -30%, -50%)
    
3.  Time-based condition (e.g. exit in 90 mins if not moving)
    
4.  How much size you’re using
    

### 

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/trading-psychology-basics#id-2.-avoid-overexposure)

2\. Avoid overexposure

Never size based on conviction. Size based on risk tolerance and worst-case scenario.

Ask:

1.  What if this token rugs in 10 minutes?
    
2.  Am I okay losing this amount?
    
3.  Will it affect how I behave on the next trade?
    

Don't enter any trade for more than 3% of your trading deposit.

### 

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/trading-psychology-basics#id-3.-normalize-small-losses)

3\. Normalize small losses

A stop-loss at -50% sounds bad, but it protects you from -95% rugs. Getting stopped out = part of the game.

The goal isn’t to win every trade — it’s to protect capital and stay in profit consistently form month to month. So earlier you understand that this one trade was a mistake — less you lose.

### 

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/trading-psychology-basics#id-4.-track-behavior-not-just-outcomes)

4\. Track behavior, not just outcomes

Review not only PnL, but _your own behavior_:

1.  Did you enter on time?
    
2.  Did you follow your exit strategy?
    
3.  What caused deviation?
    

Track this with a simple column in your journal:

> “Did I follow my plan?” → Yes / No

### 

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/trading-psychology-basics#id-5.-stop-trading-after-emotional-events)

5\. Stop Trading After Emotional Events

If you:

1.  Just took a big loss
    
2.  Missed a 10x pump
    
3.  Made a revenge trade
    

→ Stop.

Emotional volatility leads to poor decisions, usually disguised as “opportunities.” Take a break, review, come back neutral.

### 

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/trading-psychology-basics#id-6.-zoom-out)

6\. Zoom Out

It’s not about _this_ trade. It’s about the next 50.

The edge is statistical. One win means nothing. One loss means nothing.

But a system that works 55% of the time — with proper sizing — can make you unstoppable.

# Formulating trading strategy | atm.day docs

ATM provides entry signals based on on-chain indicators. Your job is to turn them into a repeatable system: when to enter, when to exit, and how much to risk.

### 

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/strategies-and-education/formulating-trading-strategy#id-1.-define-your-trading-objectives)

1\. Define Your Trading Objectives

Start by deciding:

*   What type of moves are you targeting?
    
    (Quick scalps, 2x flips, 10x runners, long holds)
    
*   How often do you want to trade?
    
    (Multiple times a day, once per session, few high-quality entries per week)
    
*   What’s your acceptable risk per trade?
    
    (% of capital you’re okay losing — common: 1–3%)
    

Clarity here makes everything else easier.

### 

[](https://gitbook.atm.day/strategies-and-education/formulating-trading-strategy#id-2.-choose-entry-conditions)

2\. Choose Entry Conditions

Use ATM signals to filter opportunities.

Your entry criteria should include at least 2–3 of the following:

Metric

Why It Matters

3\. Define Exit Strategy

Pick one exit rule and stick to it for at least 10–15 trades.

Exit Type

Example Rule

Fixed ROI

Sell 100% at 2x or 3x

TRTP + SL

Trailing TP at +100%, SL at -50%

Time-Based

Exit if price flat after 60–90 mins

Staggered

Sell 50% at 2x, rest at 3–5x or with trailing stop

Momentum Exit

Exit if volume or wallet flow flips negative

Make sure it fits your risk tolerance and time availability.

4\. Position Sizing

*   Decide % of capital per trade (e.g., 2%)
    
*   Avoid oversized positions based on “conviction”
    
*   Plan for 10+ trades in a row without blowing up
    

Example:

If your account = $5,000 and risk per trade = 2% → Max loss = $100 → Adjust size accordingly based on SL.

# Degen Toolkit to use with ATM | atm.day docs

ATM gives you the edge with on-chain metrics. You can also pair it with off-chain sentiment through other tools. These help you make smarter use of ATM signals, do deeper DYOR based on off-chain sentiment, and get a clearer view of the broader market.

Tool

Description

[KOLscan](https://kolscan.io/)

Tracks influencers' buys based on 150 public wallets.

[Tweetscout](https://tweetscout.io/)

Monitors tokens mentioned in influencer X posts; helps assess social signal strength, checks if X was renamed, finds tokens' teams based on their X descriptions.

[Getmoni](https://getmoni.io/)

Same as Tweetscout — helps to check X sentiment.

[Zap.xyz](http://zap.xyz/)

Tracks tokens' telegram mentions based on 2,500+ tg channels.

[Sandwiched](https://sandwiched.me/sandwiches)

Visualizes sandwich attacks and insider entries on Solana tokens — helps spot anomalies.

[Holderscan](https://holderscan.com/)

Analyzes holder distribution of Solana tokens — identifies concentration and bundle patterns.

[ATM Dune Dashboard](https://dune.com/atmday/pumpfun-pumpswap)

Dune dashboard by ATM for Pump.fun/Pumpswap. Helps you to better understand the market, compare different market conditions.

[Maestro Scraper](https://docs.maestrobots.com/maestro-scraper/scraper-setup)

Might help you to automate trading with ATM signals if you'll decide to give it a try (note: suggest to start from trading on paper first)
