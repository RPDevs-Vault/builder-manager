Turn services on or off for users
=================================

When you sign up for Google Workspace, you and your team get access to a host of services and applications. These include core services like Gmail and Drive, additional services like YouTube, and apps you purchase from the Google Workspace Marketplace.

Here are ways you can customize and control how users access their services.

*   [Turn a service on or off for Google Workspace users](https://support.google.com/a/answer/182442?hl=en&ref_topic=28974)
    
*   [Customize service access using access groups](https://support.google.com/a/answer/9050643?hl=en&ref_topic=28974)
    
*   [Turn on or off additional Google services](https://support.google.com/a/answer/181865?hl=en&ref_topic=28974)
    
*   [View which apps are turned on for a user, group, or organizational unit](https://support.google.com/a/answer/7541261?hl=en&ref_topic=28974)
    
*   [Add a paid subscription to your account](https://support.google.com/a/answer/45690?hl=en&ref_topic=28974)

# Customize a Google Workspace service URL - Google Workspace Admin Help

[Skip to main content](https://support.google.com/a/answer/53340?hl=en&ref_topic=29260#search-form)

Customize a Google Workspace service URL
========================================

You can make it easy for users to find your core Google Workspace services by creating a custom web address (URL) for each service. Create custom addresses in your primary and secondary domains for services including Gmail, Calendar, Google Groups, Sites, and others.

For example, with a custom address, users can sign in to their Gmail inbox at _mail.example.com_ instead of the longer default address _mail.google.com/a/example.com_. Or they can sign in to their Google Groups app at _groups.example.com_, their Calendar at _calendar.example.com_, and so on.

**If you purchased your domain from a Google partner** when you signed up for Google Workspace, you don't need to create custom addresses for Gmail, Calendar, Drive, Sites, or Google Groups. We've customized these addresses for you.

When you or your users open a custom URL, the browser displays the original Google Workspace service URL.

Customize a service address
---------------------------

1.  [Sign in](https://admin.google.com/)
     with an _administrator_ account to the Google Admin console.
    
    If you aren’t using an administrator account, you can’t access the Admin console.
    
2.  Go to Menu ![](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/JxKYG9DqcsormHflJJ8Z8bHuyVI5YheC0lAp)![and then](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/Th2Tx0uwPMOhsMPn7nRXMUo3vs6J0pto2DTn)![](https://storage.googleapis.com/support-kms-prod/BfsCvvcJ5e5ZDzwCprbZRYyyXxvBWIP3YFkY) [Account > Account settings > Custom URLs](https://admin.google.com/ac/accountsettings/customurl)
    .
    
    Requires having the [Service Settings administrator privilege](https://support.google.com/a/answer/1219251#service_settings)
    .
    
3.  (Optional) Select the domain you want to update from the list.
4.  Under a service URL, enter a prefix.  
    **Note:** Example prefixes for Google services include _calendar_, _drive_, _mail_, _groups_, and _sites_. If the prefix is already assigned to another service, remove or rename the prefix and click **Save**. Then add the prefix to the service URL.
5.  Click **Save**.
6.  Choose one:
    *   ​​​​​Follow the instructions to add a CNAME record for each service address you're customizing. Then go to step 7.
    *   Click **I'll do this later**, which cancels your changes.
7.  Sign in to your account at your domain host's website (not Google) and add a CNAME record to your domain's DNS records.
    
    **Note:** For example, to create the Gmail address _mail_.example_._com, add a CNAME record that points _mail_ to **ghs.googlehosted.com.** Your domain host records will be something like this:
    
    |     |     |     |
    | --- | --- | --- |
    | **Type** | **Name or Host** | **Value or Address** |
    | CNAME | mail | ghs.googlehosted.com |
    | CNAME | calendar | ghs.googlehosted.com |
    | CNAME | groups | ghs.googlehosted.com |
    
      
    For more information, go to [Add a CNAME record to your domain's DNS records](https://support.google.com/a/answer/47283)
    .  
8.  In the Admin console, click **I've completed these steps**.

Troubleshoot
------------

If you get an error message, check the following:

*   The custom URL may be in use as a customized Google Sites address. For details, go to [Map a new URL to your Google site](https://support.google.com/a/answer/7392423)
    .
*   You entered duplicate URLs.
*   **If your domain uses a security measure that requires HTTPS connections**, such as as [HTTP Strict Transport Security](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HTTP_Strict_Transport_Security)
    , you can't customize service addresses for your domain. The Admin console supports only HTTP connections for custom URLs. You can check the HTTP Strict Transport Security status for your domain at [https://hstspreload.org](https://hstspreload.org/)
    .
