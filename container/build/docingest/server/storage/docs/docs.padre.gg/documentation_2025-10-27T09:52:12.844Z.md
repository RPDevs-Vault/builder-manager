# Security | Padre

At Padre, we strike the balance between strong security and smooth trading experience.

Padre wallets are fully non-custodial, secured by Turnkey's scalable infrastructure for managing private keys across blockchains. With air-gapped architecture, it ensures robust security and protection from vulnerabilities.

Our app uses **2FA + private key encryption with user generated password** (team has no way to access private keys, no bots offer this!). Your password isn’t just for logging in — it plays a critical role in protecting your assets.

**How it works:**

*   **Private Key Encryption** When you import a wallet with a private key, it's encrypted using the password you set. This encryption ensures that your private key is never stored in a way that Padre can access. Even if someone gains access to your account, they can’t trade or move funds without your password.
    
*   **Turnkey Infrastructure** We use Turnkey — a secure private key infrastructure developed by Coinbase’s custody team — to manage keys safely and prevent unauthorized access. This infrastructure ensures that private keys are managed in a way that keeps them secure and inaccessible to Padre or its team.
    

**Your password acts as a second layer of defense and custom 2FA just for your private keys.** It’s used to encrypt your private key access, ensuring that even if someone gains access to your account, they can't take any actions without it. Knowing the password alone isn’t enough either — it only works if you’re also signed in.

> Padre never stores or has access to your password. Your assets stay safe — even from us.

If you have questions or want to dive deeper into how this works, reach out to the Padre team anytime.

# Glossary | Padre

Term

Definition

Base currency

Currency being traded in a market pair (e.g., in PEPE/WETH, PEPE is the base currency).

Insider

Wallet that traded the token in block 0 (bundle) or received it from another insider.

Liquidity

Total value of base and quote currencies available for trading in a market.

Slippage

Difference between the expected price of a trade and the price at which the trade is executed. Execution price might be higher or lower due to factors like low liquidity or token taxes.

Quote currency

Currency used to trade for the base currency (e.g., in PEPE/WETH, WETH is the quote currency).

Token Tax

% of each trade charged as fee by the token developer.

Tip

SOL paid directly to block builders to prioritize transaction inclusion, often signaling organic (non-botted) volume.

**What is the Trending page?**

**Trending** tab showcases tokens that are experiencing significant price changes and trading volume, helping users spot high-momentum assets quickly. This page is particularly useful for identifying tokens that are gaining popularity or seeing a surge in trading activity.

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252F93P9Izb9iprqyrSgUklO%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D737690b2-104a-4fb3-85cf-11fa40333cb6&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=8cc74f16&sv=2)

# Exit Strategies | Padre

Padre advanced **Exit Strategies** enable users to automate trade exits based on predefined conditions. This feature offers flexibility, allowing you to manage exits based on profit and loss thresholds.

It's available for both **Market Buy** and **Buy Dip Limit Orders -** ideal for adapting to various trading strategies. Once you place a market or limit buy with this feature enabled, the exit strategy values you set will be implemented.

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252FTldvDFmrpr2QJWSvPxoY%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D26e7a157-cf5e-490b-915e-f7f8f0671216&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=a32b45d7&sv=2)

### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/exit-strategies#how-to-set-up-exit-strategy)

How to set up Exit Strategy?

When placing an order, enable the **Exit Strategy** checkbox to activate the configuration panel.

You can define up to **five individual exit conditions**, each consisting of:

*   **Trigger type** – TP (Take Profit) or SL (Stop Loss)
    
*   **Price change** – the percentage change (positive for TP, negative for SL) that will trigger the sell
    
*   **Amount** – the percentage of your total position to be sold if the trigger is reached
    

Each condition is executed independently. For example:

*   TP +20%, 30% amount → sells 30% of your position if the price increases by 20%
    
*   SL -10%, 40% amount → sells 40% if the price drops by 10%
    

You may configure any combination of TP and SL conditions - up to 5 individual orders. You can leave part of the position unallocated or include overlapping conditions.

### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/exit-strategies#exit-strategy-execution)

Exit Strategy execution

*   Once the order is placed, the defined exit strategy is applied automatically.
    
*   Exit orders are submitted as **market sells** at the time conditions are met.
    
*   Exit strategies are only applied to the specific buy order they are configured with.
    
*   You can review or modify exit settings before placing the order. After confirmation, they cannot be edited.

Sell initials

# Custom Presets | Padre

Trading Presets in **Padre** allow users to **predefine transaction settings** for faster and more efficient trades. Custom presets provide flexibility in configuring priority, tip, slippage, and MEV protection settings.

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252FjDSsQMByy18IONF2gPhc%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D03df54a0-f555-4b7c-99e6-6bb362f8806d&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=be58d4b8&sv=2)

* * *

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/custom-presets#preset-options)

**Preset Options**


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Padre provides **three customizable presets** and an **adaptive dynamic mode**:

*   **P1, P2, P3**: Fully customizable presets that allow users to set:
    
    *   **Priority (Prio)**: Base fee to prioritize your transaction.
        
    *   **Tip**: Additional incentive for builders to accelerate processing (Solana & BNB only).
        
    *   **Slippage**: Maximum allowed price variation for execution.
        
    *   **MEV Protection**: Toggle to reduce the risk of front-running (not available on Base).
        
    
*   **🔥 Inferno Mode (for Solana)**:
    
    *   A **dynamic preset** that **automatically adjusts** priority and tip settings to remain competitive against rival bots.
        
    *   It uses **multi-send routing** to maximize execution speed by distributing transactions to top builders.
        
    *   Inferno Mode is **pre-configured** and **not user-adjustable**.
        
    

**P1, P2, and P3** can be fully customized by the user, while **Inferno Mode** is adaptive and automatically optimized by the system.

* * *

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/custom-presets#configuring-trading-presets)

**Configuring Trading Presets**


---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Presets can be adjusted according to **chain-specific settings**:

#### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/custom-presets#solana-and-bnb)

**Solana & BNB**

*   **Prio**: Base priority fee, set in **SOL or BNB**.
    
*   **Tip**: Optional builder tip to further incentivize priority processing.
    
*   **Slippage**: Maximum allowed price deviation for execution (e.g., **20%**).
    
*   **MEV Protection**: Toggle to reduce the risk of front-running and sandwich attacks.
    

#### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/custom-presets#ethereum-and-base)

**Ethereum & Base**

*   **Prio**: Priority fee in **Gwei** (e.g., **3 Gwei**).
    
*   **Slippage**: Maximum allowed price deviation for execution (e.g., **20%**).
    
*   **MEV Protection**: Available for Ethereum to prevent frontrunning.
    
*   **Tips are not applicable on Ethereum/Base.**
    

Once settings are configured, the preset is automatically applied when placing a trade.

* * *

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/custom-presets#inferno-mode)

**Inferno Mode**


---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252FKWAklPs85UMB6yUPHdD3%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3Da87e8814-78b2-41b8-a761-209eb197416b&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=f36f7775&sv=2)

Inferno Mode is an **advanced transaction execution system** that ensures your trades get processed **as fast as possible while keeping costs efficient**. It dynamically adjusts **priority fees and tips**, routing transactions through multiple builders for the highest success rate.

* * *

### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/custom-presets#how-it-works)

**How It Works?**

*   **Multiple transactions are sent simultaneously** to top Solana builders (bloXroute, NextBlock, Temporal, and Jito).
    
*   **Only one transaction will be successful** – a **custom Solana program ensures that the fastest transaction is finalized**, while the rest are automatically discarded.
    
*   **Builders only charge tips for successful transactions**. If a transaction is reverted, only the **priority fee (Prio) is paid**, reducing unnecessary costs.
    
*   **Padre optimizes overall spend on prio/tip**, allocating more SOL to tips to prioritize successful execution.
    

**Example** Four transactions are sent:

*   _Temporal_ is first to include the txn, getting priority + tip. The builder earns $0.5 (tip + prio).
    
*   _bloXroute_ loses but reverts the txn. The builder earns $0.2.
    
*   _NextBlock_ loses but does not revert the txn (prio is $0.01 and tip is $0.49—prio alone isn't attractive enough) - no added cost.
    
*   _Jito_ is sent in non-reverting mode - no added cost.
    

Total cost for the txn in this scenario: **$0.5 + $0.2 = $0.7**

### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/custom-presets#benefits)

**Benefits:**

✔ **Maximizes success rate** – transactions are routed across multiple builders for the best execution. ✔ **Ensures faster fills** – automatic priority adjustments keep you ahead of the competition. ✔ **Minimizes costs** – only successful transactions incur full fees, while reverts have **minimal impact**. ✔ **Prevents failed trades** – reducing congestion-related failures, especially in high-demand periods.

Inferno Mode can be enabled in the **trading widget** when placing orders on Solana.

### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/custom-presets#when-to-use-inferno-mode)

**When to Use Inferno Mode?**

*   **During high-demand events** (e.g., token launches, low-liquidity trades).
    
*   **When competing against bots** that prioritize speed.
    
*   **For critical trades** where execution speed is essential.
    

* * *

With **Trading Presets**, including **Inferno Mode**, you can **optimize your trading strategy** on Padre by predefining your settings, ensuring efficient and competitive order execution.

🔴 **Trailing Stop Loss (TSL) Order**


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A **Trailing Stop Loss (TSL)** is a dynamic exit strategy that adjusts as the price moves in your favor. It allows traders to lock in profits while limiting downside risk by maintaining a stop price at a set percentage below the peak price.

#### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/limit-orders#how-it-works)

**How It Works?**

*   if the price increases, the trigger price moves up with it
    
*   if the price drops, the trigger price stays put
    
*   once it hits the trailing stop level, the trade exits automatically
    

**Example: you buy at $100 with a** **5% trailing stop****. When price rises to $120, Stop Loss moves to $114 (5% below). If price drops to $114, you exit with profit.**

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252Fpd7nQQNexyeBdz5jIk6L%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D2ccb2240-c193-4ee5-ad0c-752b77dc31a3&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=d5c0628a&sv=2)

3.  **Set the Trailing Delta (%)**
    
    *   Enter the percentage below the highest price at which you want the stop loss to trigger.
        
    *   Example: If the **Trailing Delta is set to 5%** and the token price peaks at **$1.00**, your stop price is set at **$0.95**. If the price rises to **$1.20**, the stop adjusts to **$1.14**. If the price then drops to **$1.14**, the order executes.

    *   Enter the exact quantity of tokens to sell when the stop price is triggered.
        
    *   Use **quick-select buttons** (25%, 50%, 75%, 100%) or manually input a **custom percentage of your total holdings**.
        
    
5.  **Review Gas & Transaction Settings**

By setting a **Trailing Stop Loss**, you allow your trade to **capture gains automatically** while ensuring that if the price reverses beyond a certain threshold, you **exit at a predetermined level**.

Practical Guide: Position-Based Limit Orders vs. Fixed Limit Orders


----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When setting limit orders, traders often face a dilemma: fixed price targets vs. dynamic profit-taking. **Standard limit orders** remain fixed in size, while **Position-Based Limit Orders** adjust automatically as your holdings change. But what happens when you _buy more_ or _sell_ before your limit order executes? Does your original Take Profit (TP) or Stop Loss (SL) still work as expected? Let’s break it down with a real example and see how Fixed vs. Position-Based Limit Orders behave when your position changes.

### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/limit-orders#example-1-buying-more-before-tp-hits)

**Example 1:** Buying More Before TP Hits

1.  You buy 1,000 $SAMPLE at $0.005 each.
    
2.  You set a **Take Profit (TP) order at +100%** (sell 1,000 $SAMPLE at $0.01).
    
3.  Later, you buy another 1,000 $SAMPLE at $0.006 before the price reaches $0.01.
    

#### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/limit-orders#option-1-normal-limit-order-fixed-size)

**Option 1:** Normal Limit Order (Fixed Size)

_Your original TP order remains 1,000 $SAMPLE at $0.01._

✔️ If the price reaches $0.01, you will sell 1,000 $SAMPLE.

❌ But now you have 2,000 $SAMPLE total, meaning your TP order only covers 50% of your position.

❌ If you want to sell all your holdings, you have to manually adjust your TP order or create a second one.

**Issue:** Since the TP is attached to a fixed amount (1,000 $SAMPLE), it does not account for the fact that you now own more.

#### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/limit-orders#option-2-position-based-limit-order-dynamic)

**Option 2:** Position-Based Limit Order (Dynamic)

_Instead of a fixed amount, you set your Take Profit as a % of your total position._

✔️ When you buy more, your TP order automatically scales up.

✔️ Instead of keeping the sell amount fixed, the percentage of your position remains the same.

✔️ If price reaches $0.01, all your holdings (2,000 $SAMPLE) will be included in the TP execution.

**Benefit**: Your profit-taking strategy adjusts automatically, so you don’t have to manually update your order when you increase your position.

#### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/limit-orders#key-takeaways)

Key Takeaways

*   Use Normal Limit Orders when you want to **sell a fixed amount, regardless of new buys.**
    
*   Use Position-Based Limit Orders when you scale into a trade, so your profit target always covers your full position.
    

* * *

### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/limit-orders#example-2-selling-part-of-your-position-before-tp-hits)

**Example 2:** Selling Part of Your Position Before TP Hits

1.  You buy 1,000 $SAMPLE at $0.005 each.
    
2.  You set a Take Profit (TP) order at +100% (sell 1,000 $SAMPLE at $0.01).
    
3.  Later, you manually sell 100 $SAMPLE before the price reaches $0.01.
    

#### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/limit-orders#option-1-normal-limit-order-fixed-size-1)

Option 1: Normal Limit Order (Fixed Size)

✔️ If the price reaches $0.01, the order will attempt to execute.

❌ But after selling 100 $SAMPLE manually, your total position is now 900 $SAMPLE, while your limit order is still trying to sell 1,000.

❌ Your order will fail because it’s based on a fixed amount that no longer exists.

**Issue**: Since the order is attached to the original 1,000 $SAMPLE, it does not adjust when you partially exit your position.

#### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/limit-orders#option-2-position-based-limit-order-dynamic-1)

Option 2: Position-Based Limit Order (Dynamic)

_Instead of selecting an exact amount to sell, you set a Take Profit at +100% of your average entry._

✔️ The system dynamically adjusts your limit order size to match your current holdings.

✔️ If you sell 100 $SAMPLE manually, the Take Profit order updates automatically to reflect your remaining 900 $SAMPLE.

✔️ No failed orders. No need to manually adjust the sell amount.

**Benefit**: Your profit-taking strategy stays intact, even if you scale in or out before reaching your target.

#### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/trading/limit-orders#key-takeaways-1)

Key Takeaways

*   Use Normal Limit Orders when you want to sell a fixed amount at a fixed price, regardless of position changes.
    
*   Use Position-Based Limit Orders when **you scale in/out of a trade and want your profit target to update dynamically.**
    

With Position-Based Limit Orders, your TP order stays proportional to your actual holdings, preventing execution gaps.

# New Pairs | Padre

[https://trade.padre.gg/new-pairs](https://trade.padre.gg/new-pairs)

This tab displays newly launched tokens on the platform, giving users a quick view of recent listings. It's useful for discovering new assets in the early stages of trading. Each token entry includes key metrics, and icons indicate the blockchain and launchpad (e.g., **PumpFun**), helping users identify the token’s origin.

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252FZuSCkQujnWgHxmd92ar0%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3Db5167e60-72ca-4300-9a60-996c192af3f1&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=ca18f96e&sv=2)

*   **Asset**: Displays the name and logo of the new token. Icons may be present next to the token name:
    
    *   **Chain Icon**: Shows the blockchain network where the token is listed (e.g., Ethereum, BNB).
        
    *   **Launchpad/Protocol Icon**: Indicates the launchpad where the token was introduced, such as PumpFun/Uniswap, to provide context on its origin.
        
    
*   **Age**: Shows how recently the token was launched (e.g., "6s" for six seconds ago), helping users spot the newest tokens at a glance. Sorting by age allows users to focus on the latest listings.
    
*   **1H Price Δ (1-Hour Price Change)**: Reflects the percentage change in the token’s price over the past hour. Positive values are displayed in green, while negative values are in red, providing a quick sense of price momentum shortly after launch.
    
*   **Price USD**: Shows the current price of the token in USD, giving users a reference point for valuation.
    
*   **Trades**: Indicates the number of trades executed for each token, with a green bar for buys and a red bar for sells. This visual representation allows users to assess trading activity and buying/selling pressure at a glance.
    
*   **Volume USD**: Reflects the total trading volume in USD, providing insight into the market interest and liquidity of each new token.
    
*   **FDV USD (Fully Diluted Valuation)**: Shows the token’s fully diluted valuation, calculated based on the total supply of tokens at the current price. This metric helps users understand the potential market cap if all tokens were in circulation.
    

### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/new-pairs#filters)

**Filters**

The **Filter** button allows users to narrow down the list of new pairs based on criteria such as trading volume, price change, or specific blockchain networks. This feature is helpful for users looking to focus on specific types of new tokens.

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252FGACFVWOIIj7IpRaAiGCf%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D952e1491-c6d8-421b-b68d-d27155358a92&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=b99b34a5&sv=2)

The filter modal allows users to refine the list of new token pairs by applying specific criteria. It includes options to set volume, market cap, trade counts, and network preferences, making it easier to identify tokens that meet particular trading or investment criteria.

#### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/new-pairs#sections-and-options)

Sections and Options:

*   **Manual / Saved**:
    
    *   **Manual**: Allows users to manually set filtering criteria.
        
    *   **Saved**: Users can save frequently used filters for quick access. This tab will display any previously saved filter settings.
        
    
*   **Volume**:
    
    *   Set a range for the trading volume in USD. This filter helps users find tokens with a minimum or maximum trading volume, which can be useful for identifying either highly active or quieter new pairs.
        
    
*   **Market Cap**:
    
    *   Allows users to define a range for the token’s market capitalization. A lower market cap might indicate a newer or riskier token, while a higher market cap could signal stability and adoption.
        
    
*   **Sells / Buys**:
    
    *   Filters by the number of sell or buy transactions within a specified range. This can help users identify tokens with more buying activity (potentially indicating demand) or selling pressure.
        
    
*   **Networks**:
    
    *   Choose one or multiple blockchain networks (e.g., Ethereum, Binance Smart Chain, Solana). Selecting specific networks helps users focus on new pairs available on preferred chains.
        
    
*   **DEXes**:
    
    *   Select from various decentralized exchanges (DEXes) where these tokens are traded. Users can filter to see tokens listed only on specific platforms, such as Uniswap or PancakeSwap.
        
    

#### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/new-pairs#actions)

Actions:

*   **Reset**:
    
    *   Clears all selected filter criteria, allowing users to start fresh with no filters applied.
        
    
*   **Save**:
    
    *   Saves the current filter settings under the **Saved** tab for future use, enabling quick access to frequently used filters.
        
    
*   **Apply**:
    
    *   Activates the selected filters and displays only those new pairs that meet the criteria in the **New Pairs** tab.

This tab displays all active, unfilled orders for the selected token, allowing users to monitor pending transactions.

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252FP9v2rV1HBFksaPLZvAwc%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D8e1198df-f119-47a1-8e0f-c1ccfbd4a85e&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=e0d5f5c9&sv=2)

*   **Wallet**: Indicates the wallet used for the order (e.g., "Name 1"), helping users keep track of which wallet placed each order. The associated chain icon is also shown next to the wallet name.
    
*   **Pair**: Displays the trading pair for the order (e.g., **BULLY/SOL**), specifying which tokens are involved in the trade.
    
*   **Type**: The order type (e.g., **Buy Dip**) defines the type of order strategy used, such as market orders, limit orders, or specific strategies like “Buy Dip.”
    
*   **Side**: Indicates whether the order is a **Buy** or **Sell**. This example shows a **Buy** order.
    
*   **Price**: The specified price at which the user intends to buy or sell the token (e.g., $0.04644). This is the target price for the order to be executed.
    
*   **Amount**: Shows the quantity of the token involved in the order. If the amount is unspecified or pending, it may be left blank until filled.
    
*   **Total $**: Reflects the total dollar value of the order based on the specified price and amount. It can be useful for estimating the order's financial impact.
    
*   **Total SOL**: Displays the equivalent amount in SOL (or other relevant blockchain currencies) for the transaction, giving an alternative view of the order’s value.
    
*   **Status**: Indicates the current status of the order, such as **Open/Executing** for active orders that have not yet been executed. A timer icon may represent pending status or waiting conditions.
    
*   **Cancel Option**: The **X** icon at the end of each row allows users to cancel the open order if they wish to withdraw it before it is filled.
    

The **Open Orders Tab** provides a straightforward way to manage and monitor active orders, enabling users to track price conditions, order types, and adjust or cancel orders as necessary.

# Order History | Padre

### 

[](https://docs.padre.gg/app-guide/token-page/order-history#order-history-tab)

**Order History Tab**

This tab provides a record of completed, canceled, or failed orders, helping users track their past trading activity.

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252F0v1rsCkSK7TqWovvHGmE%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D8a312c19-7915-45f4-92f1-58d60b6e3091&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=fd8722c4&sv=2)

**Hide Other Tokens**: Checking this box filters the view to display only open orders for the currently selected token, making it easier to focus on relevant trades. Disable this option to see all open orders for all tokens.

*   **Date**: Indicates how long ago the order was placed (e.g., "5m" for five minutes ago), with the option to sort by date.
    
*   **Wallet**: Shows the wallet used for each order (e.g., "Alpha Pa..."), allowing users to identify which wallet executed each transaction. Each wallet also displays the corresponding blockchain icon.
    
*   **Pair**: Displays the trading pair involved in the transaction (e.g., **BARIO/ETH**), identifying which tokens were traded.
    
*   **Type**: Describes the type of order, such as **Stop Loss**, **Buy Dip**, or **Market**, indicating the trading strategy or execution method used for the order.
    
*   **Side**: Shows whether the order was a **Buy** (green) or **Sell** (red), providing insight into the transaction’s direction.
    
*   **Price**: Displays the price at which the trade was executed. If the trade was placed at market rate, it may show "market" as the price.
    
*   **Amount**: Shows the quantity of the token involved in the transaction. If the amount is unspecified or pending, it may be left blank.
    
*   **Total $**: Reflects the total dollar value of the transaction based on the executed price and amount. This can be useful for understanding the financial impact of each order.
    
*   **Status**: Indicates the outcome of the order:
    
    *   **Ok**: The order was successfully completed.
        
    *   **Canceled**: The order was manually canceled before execution.
        
    *   **Fail**: The order failed to execute, possibly due to slippage or insufficient liquidity.
        
    

The **Order History Tab** enables users to review their past trades, understand transaction outcomes, and identify any issues that may have affected trade execution. This historical data can help users refine their trading strategies and better manage future orders.

Events

Thissection shows a history of liquidity actions, such as adding or removing liquidity for the selected token. This helps users track changes in the liquidity pool over time.

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252FbydBnBDavQjHiKpmHAUE%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3Dbbb63520-2fa3-4235-9788-11b451d15462&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=2b39b9be&sv=2)

*   **Date**: Indicates when the liquidity event took place (e.g., "15d" for 15 days ago), with the option to sort by date for easier navigation.
    
*   **Type**: Describes the type of liquidity event. Common types include:
    
    *   **ADD**: Represents liquidity added to the pool.
        
    *   **REMOVE**: Indicates liquidity removed from the pool (shown in red to highlight outflows from the pool).
        
    
*   **Amount (Token)**: Displays the amount of the token involved in the liquidity action (e.g., **11.9586 UNIBOT**), showing the exact quantity added or removed.
    
*   **Amount (ETH)**: Shows the corresponding amount of ETH (or other paired asset) involved in the liquidity event, providing insight into the scale of the action.
    
*   **Maker**: Shows the wallet address of the user who performed the liquidity action, partially obscured for privacy (e.g., "0xAe5...D176"). The chain icon next to the address denotes the blockchain network of the transaction.
    
*   **Txn (Transaction Link)**: The **blockchain explorer icon** provides a direct link to view the liquidity event on the relevant blockchain explorer (e.g., Etherscan, Solscan). This allows users to verify details of the liquidity action directly on-chain.
    

The **Events** section offers transparency around liquidity changes, helping users monitor the flow of assets in and out of the liquidity pool. This information is particularly useful for assessing liquidity stability and identifying significant actions taken by individual providers.

Providers

This section displays a ranked list of addresses that contribute liquidity to the token’s pool, showing each provider's share of the total liquidity.

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252FvtmDR4WD8ubRA8XHXJFy%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3Db1889709-5529-40ba-b714-08fea6717956&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=586e6387&sv=2)

*   **Rank**: Lists the rank of each liquidity provider based on their contribution. The top provider is marked with a **1st place star** icon, followed by lower-ranked providers.
    
*   **Maker**: Shows the wallet address of each liquidity provider, partially obscured for privacy (e.g., "0x663...b214"). The chain icon next to the address denotes the blockchain network for the liquidity (e.g., Ethereum or Solana).
    
*   **% of Total**: Indicates the percentage of the total liquidity that each provider has contributed. This value helps users assess the dominance of each provider in the liquidity pool (e.g., one provider holding 100% of the liquidity, while others have 0%).
    
*   **Amount**: Displays the exact amount of the token that each provider has contributed to the liquidity pool. This value helps to quantify each provider’s share, providing transparency on the liquidity distribution.
    
*   **Link**: The **blockchain explorer icon** (e.g., Etherscan, Solscan) provides a direct link to view the liquidity provider’s address and transactions on the relevant blockchain explorer. This allows users to verify contributions and examine the activity of each provider.
    

This **Providers** section offers insights into the distribution of liquidity for the token, showing which addresses hold significant stakes in the liquidity pool. Users can gauge the pool's decentralization and investigate specific providers through blockchain links.

# Price Trends | Padre

In the **Price Trends** section, users can view key performance indicators of a specific asset over various time intervals.

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252F4u1nOKLHNaSbh5pzAqG4%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D591453f6-6278-4bf8-9be7-769578dc95d0&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=8b58ef45&sv=2)

*   **Price Change**: This shows the percentage change in price across different intervals: 5 minutes (5M), 15 minutes (15M), 30 minutes (30M), and 1 hour (1H). Positive values are displayed in green, indicating growth, while negative values in red signify a drop in price.
    
*   **Txs (Transactions)**: Reflects the total number of transactions made within the selected timeframe, providing a sense of trading activity.
    
*   **Volume**: Indicates the total volume traded in USD, showing the level of liquidity for the asset.
    
*   **Buys and Sells**: A horizontal bar shows the ratio of buys to sells, with a breakdown of the actual number of each (e.g., 2031 buys vs. 2010 sells). This metric helps gauge market sentiment.
    

This section offers a snapshot of price movement, trading volume, and market sentiment to support informed decision-making.

How to set up Raydium Migration sniper?

1.  Find a token that is migrating and click on "Set up Snipe" button:

![](https://docs.padre.gg/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F3836151519-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FspUNoDItDjEtPNBtDZ0i%252Fuploads%252FgkLveS8A466YHTugo86p%252Fimage.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3Dd0a22cc8-5f1b-4fba-9c15-a46db74cb18b&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=afdb9742&sv=2)
