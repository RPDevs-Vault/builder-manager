# Check-In Procedure | Attendance App

**Step 1: Prepare to Check-In**

*   Open the HKU App, tap on Attendance App when you arrive at the venue for your class or event.
    

Note: Some classes or events may allow early check-in. Please confirm with your lecturer, teaching assistant, or event organizer if early check-in is permitted.

**Step 2: Check In**

*   You will be checked in automatically for the class or event if you are registered.
    
*   Click the “CHECK IN” button to record your attendance if you are not already registered.
    

[hashtag](https://userguide.hku.hk/hku-attendance/using-the-app/check-in-procedure#check-in-scenarios)

Check-In Scenarios


------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**A. Registered Event**

*   If you have registered for the event, your attendance will be **automatically recorded** when you arrive at the venue during the allowed check-in time. Your attendance record and check-in timestamp will be displayed.
    

**B. Unregistered Event (Sit-In)**

*   If you are not registered for the event but are attending as a sit-in, tap the “**CHECK IN**” button to record your attendance. Your attendance will be logged as a sit-in.
    

**Check-In Requirements**

*   You can only check in if:
    
    *   You are enrolled in the course for the lecture.
        
    *   You have registered for the tutorial group using Tutorial Sign-up.
        
    *   You have registered for the event requiring prior registration.
        
    *   You use the "**CHECK IN**" button to record attendance as a sit-in.

# Check-Out Procedure | Attendance App

Some classes or events require you to check out at the end. Please follow these steps:

**Step 1: Prepare to Check Out**

*   Remain at the venue until you are ready to check out.
    
*   Open the Attendance App when your class or event is ending.
    
*   You can check out the class or event earliest at 30 minutes before it ends.
    
*   A check-out reminder notification will appear 15 minutes before the class or event ends.
    

**Step 2: Check Out**

*   Tap the “**CHECK OUT**” button in the app to record your departure.
    
*   You can only perform check-out no later than 1 hour after the class or event ends.
    
*   You must check out before leaving the venue.

# Troubleshooting | Attendance App

**Q: What should I do if I'm unable to record my attendance successfully?**

A: If you're having trouble recording your attendance, you can try the following steps:

*   **Switch Bluetooth off and on:** Sometimes toggling Bluetooth can resolve connectivity issues.
    
*   **Close and restart the app:** Restarting the app can help refresh its functions and resolve minor glitches.
    
*   **Rescan for events:** Pull down the "Current" tab in the app to perform event scanning again.
    
*   **Change your seat:** If possible, move to another seat during breaks to see if this improves connectivity.
    

If you've tried these steps and still cannot record your attendance successfully, please contact your lecturer or teaching assistants for assistance. They can help ensure your attendance is recorded accurately.

Welcome to the User Guide for the Content Management System (CMS) of the Smart Recycling System at The University of Hong Kong (HKU). This guide is designed to assist administrative users in efficiently managing the various aspects of the Smart Recycling System, which aims to encourage recycling among HKU staff and students.

The Smart Recycling System integrates seamlessly with the HKU App, allowing users to perform recycling at designated smart recycling bins across the campus. By recycling correctly, users can earn points, which can then be redeemed for exciting gifts and rewards.

This CMS user guide covers the essential administrative functions you need to manage the system effectively. Through the CMS, you will be able to:

**Manage Events**: Create, edit, and oversee events related to recycling initiatives and campaigns.

**Manage Promotions**: Set up and monitor promotional activities that encourage more recycling and participation.

**Manage Member Information**: Access and update user profiles, including their recycling history and points balance.

**Manage Recyclables**: Define and categorize the types of recyclables accepted by the smart bins, ensuring accurate sorting and processing.

**Manage Bin Information**: Monitor and update the status and location of smart recycling bins across the campus.

# User Roles and Permission | GreenU App

In the current implementation of the CMS for the Smart Recycling System, access to the CMS is controlled and managed by the Information Technology Services (ITS) department.

If there are any changes required to the account access—such as adding a new user, removing an existing user, or modifying user details—please contact ITS directly to request the necessary changes. ITS will handle all modifications to ensure the security and integrity of the system.

User Roles At this stage, user roles are not yet implemented within the CMS. This means that all users with access to the CMS have the same level of permissions and can perform all available administrative functions. This includes managing events, promotions, member information, recyclables, and bin information.

Future Enhancements We recognize the importance of having distinct user roles and permissions to provide a more structured and secure approach to managing the CMS. In upcoming phases, we plan to discuss and define user roles in collaboration with the Estates Office. This will involve identifying different levels of access and permissions based on the specific needs and responsibilities of various users.

Some potential user roles could include:

*   **Administrator**: Full access to all CMS features and settings.
    
*   **Event Manager**: Limited to managing events and promotions.
    
*   **Member Manager**: Restricted to viewing and editing member information.
    
*   **Recycling Manager**: Focused on managing recyclables and bin information.

# What is Recycling Campaigns | GreenU App

Recycling campaigns are strategic initiatives designed to promote and enhance recycling actions among HKU staff and students. These campaigns are set up to incentivize recycling efforts by scaling up the recycling points accumulated over a specific period.

Essentially, a recycling campaign is a logical setup within the CMS that temporarily increases the points users can earn for their recycling activities, making recycling more rewarding during the campaign period. However, it is important to note that recycling campaigns are purely a backend configuration and do not have any visible presence or related information within the GreenU app. As such, users will not see any direct references to these campaigns in the app. To effectively promote recycling campaigns, administrators are encouraged to utilize the promotion function within the CMS.

# What is Promotions | GreenU App

Promotions are another critical component of the Smart Recycling System, designed to further engage and motivate HKU staff and students to participate in recycling activities.

Similar to events, promotions are created and managed by the Estates Office and are prominently displayed on the GreenU homepage. The key difference between an event and a promotion is that promotions do not include points earning. Instead, promotions focus on raising awareness, providing incentives, or offering special deals related to recycling **WITHOUT** the reward of points.

By leveraging promotions, the Estate Office can keep the community informed and engaged in ongoing sustainability efforts.

![](https://userguide.hku.hk/greenu-app/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F1196756779-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FwMvw2aKlQCpLX6lqObTk%252Fuploads%252FFIUyS2UJSVullFSd65S9%252Fwt_is_event.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3Ded0f4298-1473-4ba3-911b-f92318729821&width=768&dpr=3&quality=100&sign=4321c6c9&sv=2)

# What is Events | GreenU App

An Event refers to a specific recycling-related activity or initiative organized by the Estates Office. These events are prominently displayed on the GreenU homepage, featuring an event banner that attracts user attention. Users can click on the banner to view detailed information about the event, such as its purpose, schedule, location, and how to participate.

![](https://userguide.hku.hk/greenu-app/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F1196756779-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FwMvw2aKlQCpLX6lqObTk%252Fuploads%252FFIUyS2UJSVullFSd65S9%252Fwt_is_event.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3Ded0f4298-1473-4ba3-911b-f92318729821&width=768&dpr=3&quality=100&sign=4321c6c9&sv=2)

Areas that Events Banner will be shown

Key Features of an Event:

**Event Banner**: A visually appealing banner displayed on the GreenU homepage to promote the event.

**Event Details**: Comprehensive information about the event, including its objectives, activities involved, and participation guidelines.

**Points Earning**: Users can earn points by joining and participating in the event, which can later be redeemed for gifts and rewards

# FAQ | GreenU App

chevron-rightQ: Why can't I upload the image?[hashtag](https://userguide.hku.hk/greenu-app/help#q-why-cant-i-upload-the-image)

A: The image can be uploaded once you have saved the editing item. This is a limitation of Microsoft MDA (Model-Driven Apps). Please ensure you save your changes first, and then proceed to upload the image.

chevron-rightQ: Why is the created item not shown in the app?[hashtag](https://userguide.hku.hk/greenu-app/help#q-why-is-the-created-item-not-shown-in-the-app)

A: One of the reasons could be that the starting time is not correct. Please make sure your Microsoft account setting is in the **Hong Kong time zone**. If the problem still exists after verifying this, please contact us for further assistance.
