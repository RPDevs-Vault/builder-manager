# What Is RayBot? | RayBot | Wallet Tracker

![Page cover](https://docs.raybot.app/start/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F1876630604-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FCbb55jaA7AAtvB0Mx70g%252Fuploads%252F9sMu6ZXHlAq3zYkSkHUL%252FTest.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D9c92d8f8-52b1-44b2-8666-2f4485e301b3&width=1248&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=e6185ff2&sv=2)

🚀 **So, what is it?** **RayBot** is a next-generation tool for **traders**, **analysts**, crypto **communities**, and **teams**. It tracks in real time:

– Wallet activity – Token buys and sells – NFT activity – Contract and protocol interactions – Transfers between addresses

RayBot supports all transaction types and acts like your **personal on-chain analyst** — only faster, more precise, and 24/7. Data is collected directly from the blockchain, filtered from noise, and delivered straight to **Telegram** or **Web interface**.

Supports **Solana** and all major **EVM networks**: Ethereum, Arbitrum, Base, BSC, and more.

* * *

📊 **Quick Stats:** 🟣 1,500,000+ wallets actively tracked 🟣 400,000+ users on board 🟣 All Solana transactions processed daily

RayBot isn’t just a tracker — it’s infrastructure built for **speed**, **reliability**, and **flexibility**.

* * *

🧠 **How It Works:** RayBot is a customizable platform — not just a bot. You can tailor it for any task:

– Whale, trader & insider tracking – Monitoring hundreds or thousands of wallets – Flexible alerts filtered by amount, token, direction, wallet age, etc. – **MultiBuy**: large-scale network-wide activity analysis – Discord integration – Support for backup and **dedicated bots** for large-scale operations

📡 All of this happens **in real time**, with **minimal noise** and **maximum clarity**.

* * *

💬 **Why is RayBot ahead of others?** Because we’re building it **with the crypto community**. We listen, refine, and evolve features based on real needs of traders, analysts, and teams.

**RayBot helps you do more than just watch the blockchain — it helps you outrun the market.**

### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/getting-started/start-your-raybot-journey#how-to-launch-raybot-in-discord)

👾 How to Launch RayBot in Discord

RayBot is now available not only in **Telegram**, but also directly in **Discord**. You can add RayBot to your server, connect wallets, and manage notifications — both in private chats and group channels.

🔧 **How to Get Started:** — In **Telegram**, type the command `**/discord**` — this will generate a link to [**RayBot’s Discord profile**](https://discord.com/users/1428817627312164904)
. — Click **“+ Add App”** to add RayBot to your **Apps** or directly to a specific **Discord channel**. — Once added, click **“Message”** to open a private chat with the bot. — Type **/start** to activate RayBot — and you’re ready to go!

> _🎥 Video example — How to connect and activate RayBot in Discord_

**Note:** Free plans only support **private messages** with RayBot in Discord. Group features become available starting from the **Hobby tier** and above. If you already have an active paid plan in **Telegram**, simply use the `**/tg**` command in Discord to link your existing subscription — your plan and wallets will automatically sync across both platforms.

🧩 **Control Center:**

Just like in Telegram, all main management happens in **private messages with the bot**. Once connected, you can also use the same commands and settings inside your **Discord groups** — identical to how they work in Telegram groups.

📘 **Learn more:** All details on how to use RayBot inside Discord will be available in the section [**Interface and Buttons in Discord**.](https://docs.raybot.app/start/interface-and-buttons-in-discord/raybot-discord-in-active-development)

**Note:** Currently, some features available in Telegram are not yet supported in Discord. We’re actively working to make RayBot **fully cross-platform**, ensuring your experience in Discord matches the power and flexibility you already enjoy in Telegram.

* * *

### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/getting-started/start-your-raybot-journey#how-to-link-telegram-and-discord)

🔗 How to Link Telegram and Discord

To use a single RayBot account and share the same subscription across both platforms, you need to link your Telegram and Discord accounts.

📌 Command: `**/tg**` — connects your Discord account to your Telegram account.

After running the command, the bot will send you a personal confirmation link.

✅ Once linked, you’ll get: — A unified subscription plan (PRO / WHALE) across both platforms — A shared list of wallets and notifications — The ability to manage everything from either Discord or Telegram

### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/getting-started/start-your-raybot-journey#what-you-need-to-know-before-getting-started)

**What you need to know before getting started:**

90% of all actions happen directly in Telegram or Discord. The remaining 10% — via the web interface, accessible with the **/web** command.

**As soon as you launch the bot, a free plan is activated:**

*   Up to 10 Solana/Tron wallets
    
*   Up to 50 EVM wallets
    
*   Some limits on data and bot usage
    
*   More about plans — in the [**UPGRADE**](https://docs.raybot.app/start/interface-and-buttons-in-telegram/upgrade)
     section

🆕 Auto Add New Wallets (Solana)

Automatically adds to tracking any new wallets that receive SOL from an already tracked one (respecting your filters).

**Example:** A wallet Binance / FixedFloat with a set range of 10–30 SOL is tracked → sends 17 SOL to a new wallet → that wallet is automatically added to your list.

**Auto-cleanup:** Every hour, the bot removes auto-added wallets with a 0 SOL balance.

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/interface-and-buttons-in-telegram/wallets#auto-add-preset-how-it-works)

⚙️ Auto Add Preset — How It Works

Let’s say you’ve selected the address Bob and want to track wallets that receive funds from him. With Auto Add Preset, you can configure exactly which notifications to receive:

*   Want to see only new token creations? → Turn off all alerts except _Create Token Account_.
    
*   Only interested in purchases? → Leave only _Swaps/Buys_ enabled.
    

Additionally, inside the preset, there’s an _Auto Add New Wallets_ option:

*   If enabled — the bot will automatically track all new wallets that Bob sends funds to.
    
*   If disabled — you’ll only receive notifications for wallets that Bob has already sent funds to in the past.

**📌 Solution:** To add more wallets, upgrade your plan using the `/upgrade` command.

If you enter a name longer than 40 characters, you’ll see the error:

Copy

    name too long.

### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/interface-and-buttons-in-telegram/groups#commands-available-in-a-group)

📜 **Commands available in a group**

After adding RayBot to a group, the following commands become available for admins and users:

*   `/add ADDRESS` — add a new wallet.
    
*   `/delete ADDRESS` — remove a wallet.
    
*   `/show` — display all added wallets.
    
*   `/topic` — set the chat topic for notifications.
    
*   `/mute` / `/unmute` — disable or enable notifications.
    
*   `/allowadmins` — allow only admins to manage wallets.
    
*   `/allowall` — allow all members to manage wallets.
    
*   `/switch` — switch RayBot in this group.
    
*   `/settings` — open the settings menu.
    
*   `/block ADDRESS` / `/unblock ADDRESS` — block or unblock a wallet.
    
*   `/dp TOKEN` — check Dex payment for a token.
    
*   `/scan_on` / `/scan_off` — enable or disable auto-scanning of tokens.
    

**Important:** Although these commands are available inside the group, the main management of RayBot is always done through another bot that is not a member of the group. `/start → Main Menu → Groups → [Your group]`

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/interface-and-buttons-in-telegram/groups#add-discord)

👾 **Add Discord**


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The **Add Discord** feature lets you connect RayBot to your Discord server and receive notifications directly in the selected channel.

### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/interface-and-buttons-in-telegram/groups#how-to-connect-raybot-to-discord)

🔑 **How to connect RayBot to Discord**

1️⃣ **In Telegram:**

*   Go to: `/start → Groups → Add Discord`
    
*   Paste the Webhook link into the chat with RayBot.
    

2️⃣ **In Discord:**

*   Open the server where you want to connect the bot.
    
*   Go to **Server Settings**.
    
*   Navigate to **Integrations → Webhooks**.
    
*   Click **Create Webhook** → enter a name and choose the channel where alerts will appear.
    
*   Click **Copy Webhook URL**.
    

3️⃣ **Final step:**

*   Return to the Telegram chat with RayBot and paste the copied Webhook URL.
    
*   The bot will now start sending notifications to the selected channel.
    

> _🎥 Video example — Watch how to add RayBot to Telegram in under 1 minute_

*   Create a separate Discord channel for trading signals.
    
*   Configure filters so only certain types of notifications appear (e.g., only buys/sells).
    
*   For convenience, you can connect multiple Webhook channels on one server (e.g., one for Swaps, another for NFTs)
    

⚙️ **Important limitation** Managing bots in Discord (adding/removing wallets, configuring filters and notifications) is done exclusively through RayBot in Telegram. In Discord channels, the bot functions only as a _notification broadcaster_.

# Telegram Rate Limits | RayBot | Wallet Tracker

We’ve been working on ways to make your experience better, especially when it comes to **rate limits**. These are rules Telegram uses to stop bots from overloading the system. Here’s a simple breakdown of what they are, why they exist, and what you can do to handle them.

* * *

**What Are Rate Limits?**

Rate limits are rules that limit how many messages a bot can send to keep Telegram running smoothly and prevent spam.

Here’s how it works:

*   **1 message per second** per bot to each user.
    
*   **20 messages per minute** per bot in a group.
    
*   About **40 messages per minute** per bot in direct messages (DMs).
    

If a bot goes over these limits, Telegram blocks it from sending messages for a while. This block could last anywhere from a few seconds to a few hours.

* * *

**Why Do These Limits Matter?**

These limits aren’t just rules—they help Telegram stay fast and reliable for everyone. To make sure we don’t run into problems, we’ve added our own controls to follow these limits. This way, you can still get your alerts without interruptions.

* * *

**What Should You Do If You See Rate-Limit Messages?**

If you ever see a message saying your alert was delayed or dropped because of rate limits, don’t worry. Here’s how you can fix it:

1.  **Use Filters to Cut Down on Notifications**
    
    *   Set up filters so you only get the alerts you really need. This reduces the total number of notifications.
        
    
2.  **Split Wallets Across Bots or Groups**
    
    *   You can use different bots or groups to spread out your wallets. For example, put one wallet in "Blue RayBot" and another in "Red RayBot."
        
    
3.  **Set Up a Backup Bot**
    
    *   Use the `/backup` command to add a backup bot (only in DMs). This bot will step in if the main one hits its limit.
        
    

* * *

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/faq-1/telegram-rate-limits#safe-mode-to-prevent-group-rate-limits)

🆘 Safe Mode to Prevent Group Rate Limits

Telegram recently added new “spam” filters that can rate-limit bots sending messages with many external links in groups.

To help prevent this, we’ve introduced **Safe Mode**. If your group was recently rate-limited by Telegram, Safe Mode will automatically turn on:

*   🚫 Disables all external links
    
*   🔹 Removes the # sign before tickers
    
*   ✅ Keeps buttons intact
    

You can also toggle Safe Mode manually in your groups at any time:

*   `/safe_on` – turn it on
    
*   `/safe_off` – turn it off
    

* * *

**What We’re Doing to Help**

We’re working hard to make sure rate limits cause as little trouble as possible. If you have any questions or run into issues, let us know—we’re here to help.

# Wallet Tracking: Core Principles | RayBot | Wallet Tracker

To use RayBot as a real analytical tool, it’s important not just to monitor random addresses, but to build a systematic approach to selecting and analyzing wallets. Below are the key principles on how to do it.

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#id-1.-why-track-wallets-at-all)

1\. Why Track Wallets at All?


---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Wallet tracking gives you real-time access to capital movements. Instead of analyzing charts after the fact, you see where the money starts to flow as it happens.

### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#this-provides-several-advantages)

This provides several advantages:

*   **Understanding capital behavior.** You can see where and when money moves, and how different market participants react.
    
*   **Predicting market sentiment and trend shifts.** Capital always moves first. Wallet transactions signal an emerging or reversing trend before it becomes visible on the chart.
    
*   **Discovering new tokens and trends before they go mainstream.** When a “smart” wallet buys an obscure token, it’s worth paying attention — especially if multiple wallets do the same.
    
*   **Understanding the strategies of top traders.** By tracking the wallets of top traders, you can start spotting patterns: how much they invest and how confident they are. Small buys often suggest caution or just gambling, while DCA or rapid accumulation of large amounts is a sign of strong conviction.
    
*   **Detecting mass and whale activity before price moves.** A timely signal from a verified wallet or a group of wallets can give you a lead of several minutes or hours — often enough to make a move before the crowd.
    
*   **Filtering out market noise.** The market is full of random movements, but by tracking verified wallets, you can focus only on what truly matters.
    

**Example from the community:** Everyone remembers the launch of the $Trump token that shook the global crypto scene and beyond. At that time, many tokens with that name appeared, but one of them quickly stood out due to a sharp influx of capital. Here’s the screenshot that was shared with us:

![](https://docs.raybot.app/start/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F1876630604-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FCbb55jaA7AAtvB0Mx70g%252Fuploads%252F6u5R80CaNUpVPbs4a4jh%252FScreenshot%25202025-07-21%2520at%252019.44.27.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D72a0dcc1-e688-4de1-bce3-a767b525844b&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=44370f85&sv=2)

With a high-quality wallet list, the first signal came in at a $196M market cap. The token later reached $15B, and the Total Buy metrics showed anomalous volumes — a clear sign that big money was coming in.

Here’s the key: Those using RayBot noticed the token in its very early stages, right when capital started flowing in. Everyone else heard about it later through news, social media, and public channels.

Who won here? Clearly, those who were prepared and caught the money flow in time. In skilled hands, RayBot isn’t just a tool — it’s a real money-printing machine. In these situations, speed and timely information make all the difference.

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#id-2.-number-of-wallets-balancing-breadth-of-coverage-and-data-quality)

2\. Number of Wallets: Balancing Breadth of Coverage and Data Quality


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The more wallets you track, the broader your understanding of the market. But there’s a catch: too many sources can turn the notification feed into noise, making it easy to miss what really matters.

### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#when-to-track-hundreds-of-wallets)

When to Track Hundreds of Wallets:

If your goal is to gauge the overall “temperature” of the market — how the masses behave, where liquidity is flowing, and which tokens are starting to catch broad attention. This approach helps build a global context and quickly assess market interest in specific tokens or trends.

### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#when-to-narrow-down-your-selection)

When to Narrow Down Your Selection:

If you want clean, high-quality signals from strong traders, it’s better to limit your focus to 20–50 vetted wallets and their sub-wallets that consistently deliver positive results. These wallets usually operate with a clear strategy or thesis behind their moves, turning their activity into meaningful signals rather than random buys.

**Note:** It’s important to remember that even top traders make mistakes. They lose money too — the on-chain market is complex, full of manipulations, false moves, and hidden pitfalls.

You shouldn’t blindly follow someone else’s actions. Always stick to your own strategy, practice proper money management, and do your own research — in other words, **DYOR (Do Your Own Research)**.

That’s the only way to turn insights from RayBot into a tool for a systematic approach rather than just another form of gambling.

Ultimately, it all depends on your trading style and desired outcomes. The crypto market has evolved significantly in recent years: those who succeeded in the past often lose their edge today because they haven’t adapted to the new landscape.

Drawing lines on charts is no longer enough — on-chain liquidity moves fast and constantly shifts. To stay on top of these flows, you need to follow the money, not just the charts.

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#id-3.-wallet-quality-key-metrics-to-watch)

3\. Wallet Quality: Key metrics to Watch


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Not every wallet provides valuable signals. When selecting wallets to track, it’s essential to look beyond just transaction history and consider trader behavior. Here are the metrics that matter:

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#number-of-transactions)

Number of Transactions

*   **High transaction count over the last 30 days** usually indicates a _spray and pray_ strategy — buying everything in hopes that something will take off. If the overall PnL is positive, this wallet can still be useful for **Multi Buy** strategies.
    
*   **Low transaction count over the last 30 days** typically reflects more deliberate decisions. These wallets tend to provide cleaner and more precise signals.
    

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#trade-sizes)

Trade Sizes

*   The size of each trade indicates the trader's level of conviction. Small bets on gamble tokens are normal. But if the trader starts making large allocations to a specific token, it signals strong confidence.
    

What counts as small, medium, or large varies by trader. Tracking wallets that only move tiny amounts back and forth is pointless — but when those same traders suddenly increase their sizes, that’s worth paying attention to.

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#pnl-profit-and-loss)

PnL (Profit and Loss)

PnL is a key metric for understanding how consistently a trader earns. Even +10% monthly returns are impressive if they compensate for previous losses. PnL helps distinguish lucky shots from traders with real, repeatable strategies.

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#wallet-activity)

Wallet Activity

If a wallet makes one or two trades and hits big, it’s likely luck or insider info. Tracking such wallets is pointless — they might be drained of funds in a few days. Instead, try to trace the funding source — you might uncover the main wallet behind the moves.

If a wallet has a rich trading history and remains active for over 30 days, it shows the trader uses it consistently. Such wallets are worth tracking.

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#purchase-history)

Purchase History

If a wallet consistently participates in hyped tokens _and profits_, that’s a good sign. It suggests the trader can distinguish solid projects from scams.

If the trader buys everything indiscriminately and loses more than they win, the wallet isn’t worth following.

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#trading-style)

Trading Style

Traders vary in style:

*   Some trade only fresh pairs, where you must take profits fast before the token "dies" in a few hours.
    
*   Others accumulate positions in well-known tokens long before they hit mainstream attention.
    

Understanding these styles helps you know under what conditions and timeframes a wallet is useful. Also, keep in mind that successful wallets often have sub-wallets — identifying them provides a fuller picture.

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/wallet-tracking-core-principles#important)

Important

To make your wallet list truly effective, you must understand each trader’s behavior: where they excel and where they falter. This allows you to filter signals and only act on those aligned with your strategy.

**Practical Example:** Say a trader actively trades small sizes of $100–500 with a 10% win rate. But when they size up to $5,000–10,000+ or start slowly accumulating a token, their win rate jumps to over 60%.

# Finding New Wallets | RayBot | Wallet Tracker

Your wallet watchlist should be updated regularly. The market evolves constantly — some traders disappear, new ones emerge, and those who once provided strong signals may go silent or become ineffective. So, the logical question is: where do you find new wallets, and how do you do it efficiently?

Let’s break the search down into key categories:

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#finding-top-and-early-traders-in-hyped-launches)

🔍 Finding Top and Early Traders in Hyped Launches

This is one of the most valuable and productive types of wallet discovery. The goal is to identify traders who consistently enter projects early and profit reliably.

You can spot them by analyzing:

*   **Top Traders** tabs
    
*   **Top Holders** tabs
    
*   **Fresh pair listings**
    
*   **Token purchase history** or signs of early accumulation before visible growth
    

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#targeted-search-for-a-specific-user)

🎯 Targeted Search for a Specific User

Sometimes the goal is to track a particular individual — an influencer, a community member, or a trader tied to a specific token or event.

This could involve:

*   Tracking wallet actions following social media posts
    
*   Analyzing entries into tokens before/after announcements
    
*   Identifying wallets managed by a single source of influence
    

These wallets are especially valuable when their owners know how to capture attention. One post — and the token comes to life. And you’re already positioned.

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#searching-for-sub-wallets)

🧩 Searching for Sub-Wallets

Most experienced traders operate multiple wallets. For example:

*   One for storage
    
*   One public (signal wallet)
    
*   One private (trading wallet)
    
*   One deposit (exchange wallet), etc.
    

To see the full picture, it's important to detect related addresses and track not just the main wallet, but also its “periphery.”

* * *

In the next sections, we’ll cover specific techniques and tools to help you quickly and accurately discover such wallets — with real examples and practical tips for working with RayBot.

* * *

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#practice-how-to-find-new-wallets-in-practice)

**🛠 Practice: How to Find New Wallets in Practice**


----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Here are some effective methods you can use to regularly discover interesting wallets worth tracking:

### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#dexscreener-how-to-find-active-wallets-through-successful-tokens)

Dexscreener: How to Find Active Wallets Through Successful Tokens

One of the simplest and most effective ways to discover new, high-potential wallets is by analyzing who participated in early buys or accumulated a position near the lows of a token that has already gained attention.

### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#step-1-choose-a-token-to-analyze)

**🔹 Step 1: Choose a Token to Analyze**

The first — and most important — step is selecting the right token around which you’ll build your analysis.

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#the-ideal-candidate-is)

✅ The Ideal Candidate Is:

*   A token that has already shown **strong growth** or is **gaining attention** in the crypto community
    
*   An asset that supports a **clear thesis** — whether it’s meme potential, influencer backing, a strong tech narrative, or a product with real-world utility
    
*   A token that has **survived its first 24 hours** and is still trading actively
    

> #### 
> 
> [](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#why-this-matters)
> 
> 🕒 Why This Matters:
> 
> Around **95% of new tokens “die”** within the first 24 hours of launch. Trading these requires serious experience, insider knowledge — or pure luck.
> 
> If a token stays alive beyond the first day, that alone is a filter. It signals potential real interest behind the project — and within the early buyers, there may be wallets worth tracking.

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#what-to-avoid)

❗ What to Avoid:

Don’t randomly pick a token that’s pumping without understanding _why_. Artificial pumps may look exciting, but more often than not they end in losses — with only the token creators and a handful of sharp traders walking away with profit (and sometimes, not even them).

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#pro-tip)

📌 Pro Tip:

If you **understand why traders are buying** a token, you can backtrack and find out **who bought early**. And _those wallets_ become your new signal sources.

### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#example)

**🔹Step 2: You’ve Chosen the Token — Now Start the Analysis**

#### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#once-youve-selected-a-token-your-next-goal-is-to-identify-the-wallets-that)

Once you’ve selected a token, your next goal is to identify the wallets that:

*   Entered _early_, before the price began to rise
    
*   Accumulated volume near the lows, ignoring the noise
    
*   Made **large buys at key moments** — before a reversal or just ahead of an impulse move
    
*   Most importantly: **closed in solid profit**
    

These are the wallets representing what’s often called **“smart money”** — traders who know how to find liquidity, react quickly to market shifts, and make consistently profitable decisions.

If you can spot them in time, you’ll be able to:

*   Sync with their signals
    
*   Understand their thesis
    
*   And in some cases, even **act before they do**
    

### 

[](https://docs.raybot.app/start/use-cases/finding-new-wallets#example-1)

📊 **Example:**

To start, you can open the **Top Traders** tab on Dexscreener and review the list of active traders.

Then, review each participant’s TXNS to manually analyze their behavior. The following patterns are what we’re looking for:

A) **Single large buys**, especially if made near the lows on the chart. This often points to a confident entry based on a clear thesis.

B) **Gradual accumulation** of the token at low levels. This suggests a strategic and planned approach, rather than impulsive trading.

**Who’s Not of Interest** 🔪 **Scalpers** — traders who jump into every pump & dump and instantly take profits are usually not valuable for long-term tracking. Their behavior is unstable, their signals noisy, and their actions rarely reflect a well-formed thesis.

🎯 **Snipers** — wallets that enter tokens in the first few blocks. These are typically part of automated _spray-and-pray_ strategies that deploy capital across hundreds of tokens, hoping one or two will deliver outsized returns. Such wallets are often backed by large budgets and provide little insight for thoughtful or thesis-driven analysis.

Hints Inside Dexscreener

Dexscreener offers many hidden but valuable cues — if you know where to look. Let’s break down which interface elements can help you quickly spot the right traders and separate noise.

*   **Bought / Sold / PnL / Unrealized Profit** Show how much the wallet has spent, its realized profit, and any unrealized gains. These are your basic indicators for evaluating a trader’s effectiveness.
    
*   **Token Balance** If the current token balance doesn’t match the purchase/sell volume, it means some tokens were received or sent via transfers. This scenario requires further analysis.
    
*   **TXNS (Number of Transactions)** The more trades, the higher the chance you’re looking at a bot or scalper. ✅ A moderate number of buy transactions is ideal. 🟠 A high number of sells is fine — it may mean the trader is taking profits in chunks, which is a smart and normal strategy.
    
*   **Address Color Indicators** 🟩 Green — wallet is in profit (realized gains) 🟥 Red — wallet is at a loss or has unrealized gains ⚠️ Unrealized profit can sometimes still show as “in red” — these wallets should be manually verified. See the screenshot below for clarity.
