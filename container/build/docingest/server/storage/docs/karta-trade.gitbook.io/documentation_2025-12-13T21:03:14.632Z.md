karta.trade is the first trading terminal with a real-time market map for Pump.fun.

Instead of scrolling through endless tokens, you see the entire market on one screen in two dimensions: age and market cap (with volume coming soon).

The idea is simple: help traders spot patterns faster, understand where real movement is happening, and trade with better context. You can clearly see the relative strength of every token compared to the whole market.

We’re in beta. The Pump.fun market is live, and prediction and perp markets will be added soon in the similar disruptive visualisation.

The map represents the entire Pump.fun market in real time.

*   X-axis → token age (minutes since launch)
    
*   Y-axis → market cap
    

Where a token appears on the map tells you a lot about its current state.

![](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F1001111530-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FVwz62MQhE9kjLvteE9Wm%252Fuploads%252FB30kAgRpjC6bmt5qFkHD%252Fintro-new-2.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D1fbcc7ae-8ad5-4cac-9ffc-3b96cd2bae27&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=de846138&sv=2)

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/discover/read-map#fresh-zone-0-45-minutes)

Fresh Zone (0–45 minutes)

Newborn tokens. Most tokens start and die here.

What to look for:

*   Clean early strength compared to same-age tokens
    
*   Fast moves toward the migration line
    

If something stands noticeably above the others in this zone, it’s often one of the earliest signs of momentum.

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/discover/read-map#runners-zone-above-the-graduation-line)

Runners Zone (above the graduation line)

Tokens above the Pump.fun graduation threshold.

What it means:

*   These tokens already broke out
    
*   They hold relative strength versus the whole market
    
*   Survival rate is higher
    

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/discover/read-map#losers-zone-older-tokens-with-low-mcap)

Losers Zone (older tokens with low mcap)

Tokens that survived in time but failed to grow.

Indicators:

*   Age is high, but market cap remains low
    
*   No clear momentum
    

This zone helps you filter out noise and avoid spending time on weak tokens.

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/discover/read-map#putting-it-together)

Putting it together

The map lets you instantly see:

*   Where the fresh opportunities are
    
*   Which tokens are actually outperforming (runners)
    
*   Which ones have already lost momentum (losers)
    
*   How each token compares to the entire market, not just its own chart
    

This is the essence of relative strength: position on the map shows whether a token is strong, average, or weak for its age.

The map gives you an instant read on the current market state — how strong or weak the field is, where momentum flows, and whether it’s a good moment to take risk. This context is crucial: it changes how you trade and what setups you look for.

![](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F1001111530-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FVwz62MQhE9kjLvteE9Wm%252Fuploads%252F5yaskUNb2nO9JcbPGY9Z%252Fstate-12-05-utc-12.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D6f1b5d42-e915-4c0b-a8a9-fe7c3bbd92f2&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=3171c2a7&sv=2)

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/discover/state-of-the-trenches#a-quick-glance-at-the-map-reveals)

A quick glance at the map reveals:

*   **How many tokens launched**
    
    Shows how “hot” the market is right now. From our own observations, there are usually 1.5–2× more launches during ET mornings and evenings (likely a better time to trade).
    
*   **How many tokens graduated**
    
    Shows if there are any runners in the current market.
    
*   **How many tokens are still above the graduation line**
    
    A unique metric you won’t find in other terminals.
    
*   **How many Mayhem tokens launched**
    
    Hint: they graduate very rarely.
    

This snapshot sets your expectations before any trade.

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/discover/state-of-the-trenches#why-this-matters)

Why this matters

#### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/discover/state-of-the-trenches#id-1.-you-can-recognize-repeating-market-patterns)

1\. You can recognize repeating market patterns

The trenches reveal patterns that consistently show up:

*   Mayhem tokens rarely graduate → when the field is full of red, the market is unstable and noisy
    
*   Best time to trade when there're more tokens stay above grad line & overall more launches happen
    

Seeing these patterns early shifts how you trade that session.

#### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/discover/state-of-the-trenches#id-2.-you-get-market-data-no-other-tool-provides)

2\. You get market data no other tool provides

karta.trade shows metrics that don’t exist in other terminals:

*   the exact number of tokens above graduation right now
    
*   how strength is distributed across ages and caps
    

This is key for timing, risk management, and understanding whether the market is expanding or shrinking.

#### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/discover/state-of-the-trenches#id-3.-it-helps-you-set-your-expectations-better)

3\. It helps you set your expectations better

Dead tokens are obvious on the map. So you better understand what should be the expectations (should you look for 100x or scalp for 15-30%).

Also helps to set the % of the deposit you want to trade with: better the market — higher the risk.

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/discover/state-of-the-trenches#bottom-line)

Bottom line

The trenches view turns the entire market into a readable structure.

You instantly see:

*   where strength lives,
    
*   whether momentum exists,
    
*   when to push risk,
    
*   and when to sit out.
    

It’s one of the fastest ways to understand the state of Pump.fun before you make a single trade.

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/trade/trade#portfolio)

Portfolio

#### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/trade/trade#open-positions)

Open positions

This tab displays every position you currently hold, with all key metrics in one view:

*   Token — the token you’re holding.
    
*   Total Profit — combined unrealized and realized PnL for this position.
    
*   Balance — your current amount of tokens.
    
*   Unrealized — profit/loss if you close right now.
    
*   Realized — profit/loss already locked in from partial sells.
    
*   Bought / Avg — total amount spent and your average entry price.
    
*   Sold / Avg — total amount sold and your average exit price.
    
*   Sell All — closes the entire position with one action.
    

This view lets you quickly understand how each trade is performing and whether you want to hold, scale, or exit.

If you bought the token and no longer see it on the map (it was launched 3+h ago) — you still can sell it through portfolio tab.

![](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2F1001111530-files.gitbook.io%2F%7E%2Ffiles%2Fv0%2Fb%2Fgitbook-x-prod.appspot.com%2Fo%2Fspaces%252FVwz62MQhE9kjLvteE9Wm%252Fuploads%252FWAfgxCffMN30NzPvc0TD%252FScreenshot%25202025-12-10%2520at%252023.21.13.png%3Falt%3Dmedia%26token%3D7145364c-55f3-4b6a-a05f-6b33cb98ee04&width=768&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=ee416b8d&sv=2)

#### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/trade/trade#closed-positions)

Closed Positions

This tab lists all positions you’ve fully exited.

For each token, you see:

*   Total Profit — your final result on the trade.
    
*   Unrealized — always 0 (position is closed).
    
*   Realized — total realized PnL.
    
*   Bought / Avg and Sold / Avg — your complete trade history for that position.
    

Closed Positions act as your trade log — a simple way to review what worked and what didn’t.

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/trade/trading-tips#start-with-the-market-not-with-a-token)

Start with the market, not with a token

Before clicking anything, read the market weather:

*   How many tokens launched in the last 3h
    
*   How many graduated
    
*   How many are still above the graduation line
    
*   How many Mayhems (they almost never graduate)
    

If there are no clear leaders and almost nothing near graduation, it’s probably not a moment to go heavy.

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/trade/trading-tips#compare-tokens)

Compare tokens

Relative strength makes the most sense inside the same age group.

*   Look at tokens around, say, 20–40 minutes old
    
*   See which ones already sit much higher in MCAP
    
*   Those are your early strength candidates; the rest is noise
    

Don’t compare a 5-minute meme to a 2-hour survivor.

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/trade/trading-tips#use-zones-not-guesses)

Use zones, not guesses

Think in zones rather than individual dots:

*   Fresh zone (0–45m) → early, high-variance plays. Look for clear outliers, not random spikes.
    
*   Runners zone (above grad line) → proven strength; better for trend-following and scaling.
    
*   Losers zone (old + low cap) → mostly avoid, unless you have a very specific idea.
    

The map turns this from a feeling into something you can literally see.

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/trade/trading-tips#let-patterns-repeat-for-you)

Let patterns repeat for you

Tokens tend to follow familiar shapes. When a token’s position and shape look like past winners, it deserves more attention than a random new dot at the bottom.

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/trade/trading-tips#use-the-map-to-size-risk)

Use the map to size risk

Market weather → position size:

*   Hot market (many graduates / strong leaders) → you can justify taking more setups or slightly larger sizes.
    
*   Cold market (no leaders / flat field) → consider smaller bets or waiting.
    

The goal is not to trade more, but to trade in sync with the environment.

If a token looks strong on the map _and_ has real volume behind it, the setup is usually cleaner than a ghost pump.

### 

[](https://karta-trade.gitbook.io/docs/trade/trading-tips#use-the-map-as-your-radar-not-your-bias)

Use the map as your radar, not your bias

The workflow:

1.  Scan the map → find 2–3 interesting tokens.
    
2.  Open them, check basics and risk.
    
3.  Take the trade (or pass), then keep an eye on how they move vs the rest of the field.
    

If your token falls back into the crowd while others keep running, that’s already a signal.
