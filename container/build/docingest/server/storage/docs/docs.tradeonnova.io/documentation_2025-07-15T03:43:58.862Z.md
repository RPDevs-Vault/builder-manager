# FAQ | Nova

What is Processor?[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/welcome/faq#what-is-processor)

`Jito` - submits transactions through Jito's block engine (bundles & mev protect) `Node` - submits transactions through our custom staked node (**no mev protect**) `Both` - submits to both jito and node concurrently If you are using jito or both, make sure to add a mev tip in settings

What is Buy & Sell Tip?[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/welcome/faq#what-is-buy-and-sell-tip)

Extra payment made to JITO's network to make sure your transaction gets through faster in the next available block over others.

What is Buy & Sell Fee?[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/welcome/faq#what-is-buy-and-sell-fee)

The fee you want to pay to get your transaction processed.[](https://docs.bloombot.app/general/tos-and-privacy-policy)

What is Slippage?[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/welcome/faq#what-is-slippage)

*   **What It Is:** The difference between the expected price and the actual price you pay for a memecoin.
    
*   **Why It Happens:** It occurs due to price fluctuations between the time you place the order and when it’s executed, especially in highly volatile memecoins.
    
*   **How to Set It:**
    
    *   If slippage is set too low, your transaction might fail.
        
    *   A higher slippage (e.g., 1%-10%) ensures the transaction goes through but may result in paying more than expected.
        
    

What is a market cap?[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/welcome/faq#what-is-a-market-cap)

Market Cap = Current Price of the Token × Total Supply of Tokens in Circulation

# Setting Up | Nova

Nova Click is easy to set up. Make sure to have full access to the Telegram bot!

Go to [Chrome Extension Workaround page](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/modules/nova-click/chrome-extension-workaround)
 to make sure you are on the most up to date version of Nova Click.

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/modules/nova-click/setting-up#video-tutorial)

Video Tutorial 🔽 -


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The guide below is for Chrome, **for firefox** use this -

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/modules/nova-click/setting-up#downloading-the-extension)

Downloading the Extension


----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

In order to use Nova Click, you will first need to have access to the bot on Telegram. Then navigate over to the Chrome Extension Store and download the Nova Extension.

[![Logo](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/~gitbook/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fssl.gstatic.com%2Fchrome%2Fwebstore%2Fimages%2Ficon_144px.png&width=20&dpr=4&quality=100&sign=7d3f0245&sv=2)Nova Click - Chrome Web Store](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/nova-click/agegahikpkeljmhlggpipmepoigaimdk)

The Official Link to the Extension

Once the extension is downloaded, open by pressing the Nova Icon in the top right & turn the extension on!

Turn off the extension, until it is connected to your Telegram. Follow the steps chronologically.

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/modules/nova-click/setting-up#setting-up-the-extension)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1

###

# Slippage | Nova

Slippage is how many less tokens you're willing to receive in any given transaction. This means if optimised, your transaction is more likely to go through.

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/slippage#what-slippage-should-i-set)

What slippage should I set?


---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Your slippage depends on the volatility of the token. We recommend a value between 50-80% for tokens trending, and lower for stable tokens.

High Slippage

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/slippage#tab-high-slippage)

Average Slippage

# MEV Protect | Nova

Turn MEV Protect on in order to prevent MEV attacks on your transactions.

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/mev-protect#what-is-mev-attacks)

What is MEV attacks?


----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A **MEV attack** on Solana happens when validators manipulate the order of transactions in a block to make extra profits. This could involve front-running trades (placing their transaction first) or back-running (placing it right after). This means you might lose out on potential profits and have to purchase the same token for much higher than you originally intended for.

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/mev-protect#how-can-i-protect-myself-from-mev-attacks)

How can I protect myself from MEV attacks?


------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Enabling MEV Protect will mean that your processor is changed to Jito instead of Ultra exclusively which will mean you wait a little longer for a MEV protected slot insuring your transaction will be sent safely.

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/mev-protect#how-do-you-turn-mev-protect-on)

How do you turn MEV Protect on?


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1

### 

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/mev-protect#click-on-settings)

Click on ⚙️ `Settings`

2

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/mev-protect#click-on-mev-protect)

Click on 🔴 `MEV Protect`

The button should toggle on : 🟢 MEV Protect. Your [processor](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/processor)
 will also change to Jito - as this protects your transaction.

# Processor | Nova

There are different modes of submitting transactions available on Nova, for different speeds and needs.

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/processor#the-different-modes)

The different modes ⬇️


----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Jito

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/processor#tab-jito)

Node

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/processor#tab-node)

Ultra

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/processor#tab-ultra)

Temporal

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/processor#tab-temporal)

NextBlock

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/processor#tab-nextblock)

Submits transactions through Jito's block engine (bundles & MEV Protects your transaction)

Submits transactions through our custom staked node **(NO MEV Protect)**

Submits to both Jito and Node concurrently

Submits to Temporal (0.001 minimum buy tip required)

Submits to NextBlock (0.001 minimum buy tip required)

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/processor#what-is-the-difference)

What is the difference?


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Jito** - As your transaction is being processed, a tip will be added to the tx for it your transaction to be included in the next block. **Allows for** [MEV Protection](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/mev-protect)
 as the next available slot can be a mev protected one.

**Node -** Your transaction is processed through our custom staked node, which may result in faster trade execution with less waiting. However, this will mean **you cannot get a mev protected slot** for your transaction, and opens you to risk of a MEV attack.

**Ultra -** Your transaction is sent in both a Jito Bundle and to our custom node. Whichever includes your transaction in the next available block gains priority.

**Temporal** - Your transaction is sent to Temporal, and processed accordingly.

**NextBlock -** Your transaction is sent to NextBlock, and processed accordingly.

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/configuration/settings/processor#how-do-i-change-processors)

Referrals are organized into a 3-tier system ✨


------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.  Tier 1 - Direct Referrals: Earn 30% of Nova’s 1% fee on every trade.
    
2.  Tier 2 - Indirect Referrals: Earn 3% of Nova's 1% fee on every trade.
    
3.  Tier 3 - Extended Referrals: Earn 2% of Nova's 1% fee on every trade.
    

💡 _See below for an explanation on how each tier works_

Tier 1 (Direct Referrals)

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/earning-with-nova/referrals#tab-tier-1-direct-referrals)

Tier 2 (Indirect Referrals)

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/earning-with-nova/referrals#tab-tier-2-indirect-referrals)

Tier 3 (Extended Referrals)

[](https://docs.tradeonnova.io/earning-with-nova/referrals#tab-tier-3-extended-referrals)

For referring people with your unique referral code - you will earn 30% of our fees from any users that came to Nova directly because of **you.**

**Example - Imagine you are Person A:**

*   You (_Person A_) share your referral link with a friend, Person B.
    
*   Person B signs up using your link and trades $10,000 in a day.
    
*   Nova's Fee: $10,000 \* 1% = $100
    
*   Your Commission (Person A) : 30% of $100 = $30
    

Users who signed up using a referral link from a user and then share their own referral - you can earn 3% of our fees from their users too.

**Example:**

*   Person B shares their referral link with Person C.
    
*   Person C signs up and trades $10,000 in a day.
    
*   Nova Bot’s Fee: $10,000 \* 1% = $100
    
*   Your Commission (Person A) : 3% of $100 = $3
    
*   Person B’s Commission: 30% of $100 = $30
    

Users who signed up using a referral link from an indirect referral (Person C) and then share their own referral - you can earn 2% of our fees from their users too.

**Example:**

*   Person C shares their referral link with Person D
    
*   Person D signs up and trades $10,000 in a day.
    
*   Nova Bot's Fee: $10,000 \* 1% = $100
    
*   Your Commission (Person A) : 2% of $100 = $2
    
*   Person B’s Commission: 3% of $100 = $3
    
*   Person C’s Commission: 30% of $100 = $30
    

* * *

Nova's generous referral structure means that even with just a handful of direct referrals, you can earn from the trading activity of multiple users at different tiers.
